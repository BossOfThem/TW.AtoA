## Decision

The Q2 world-pitch question is answered by rejecting the synthetic-braid framing in favor of **real-world cultures + native myth**. Three launch civilizations — **Greek, Aztec, Norse** — each with a famous historical commander, a 6-tower roster grounded in culturally-recognizable building types, a 5-unit roster of culturally-recognizable military classes, and a mythic endgame that unlocks through a Divinity economy and culminates in Fusion-to-Gods.

### Thematic frame (locked)

- **Real cultures, not synthesis.** Greek / Aztec / Norse as the launch civilizations. High-school-recognizable names, references, and iconography throughout. Nothing invented where a real cultural referent exists.
- **"Mundane names outside, myth inside" rule.** Tower *labels* are marketable historic building types (Pyramid, Longship Dock, Acropolis). Native myth lives *inside* the structure — stats, behaviors, upgrade paths, flavor, and fusion outputs. Avoid niche terms on the wrapper.
- **High-school recognizable test.** Every name, unit, ability, god, and resource must be something a person with a high-school degree would recognize. Specifically rejected in-session: Agoge, Molon Labe, Hot Gates (too niche). Replaced with: Spartan Training, This Is Sparta!, The Last Stand. Applied uniformly.
- **AGES concept deferred.** Age-model is **not** part of the match layer. Kept as future PvE-campaign-map concept with per-unit leveling + attributes. Revisit when PvE campaign scope opens.
- **Mechanics preserved** per 2026-04-24 reopen: TD / merge / debuff / magic.

### Fusion endgame — the ONLY mythic layer above T4 (locked)

Earlier session draft proposed standalone T4/T5/T6 tiers (Heroes/Gods/Pantheon); redirected by PM in favor of **fusion-as-endgame**: T4 Demigods fuse pairwise into Gods. No standalone T5/T6 tower tiers exist.

**Fusion shape:** Two T4+ Demigod towers of the same civ merge into **one** Fused-God tower occupying one board slot. Board consolidation is the cost. Output tower has both source attack types (see Follow-up #2 for attack-type mapping).

### Match economy (locked)

| Resource | What it is | Where it comes from | What it buys |
|----------|-----------|---------------------|--------------|
| **Tribute** | Primary currency. Historic name chosen over "gold" (mineral-named rejected by PM). | Kills + passive trickle. | Units, towers, T1→T2→T3→T4 upgrades/merges. |
| **Divinity** | Mythic token. Originally "Relic" — renamed mid-session. | Boss rounds (mini + main). | Fusion menu unlock + fusion executions. |

**Round structure:** 30 waves. Mini-boss at 5/15/25. Main boss at 10/20/30. Each boss drops 1 Divinity. **Max 6 Divinity per 30-round match.**

**Divinity spend:**
- **2 Divinity** unlock the Fusion menu civ-wide (reaches by round 10-ish with mini+main).
- **1 Divinity** per fusion execution.
- Theoretical max: 2 unlock + 4 fusions = 6 Divinity spent. Practical: 2-3 fusions per match.

### Match arc (locked)

**Lane-wars.** Destroy opponent's base. Solo variant: survive N waves or eliminate all spawns. 30-round cap is the natural match length.

## Context

The 2026-04-24 Phase 1 reopen set the thematic direction as "synthetic mythic-history braid." Q2 was to produce a world-pitch draft in that frame. Draft was filed 2026-04-25 earlier the same day as [`2026-04-25-q2-world-pitch-draft.md`](2026-04-25-q2-world-pitch-draft.md) (Proposed).

PM reacted by rejecting the braid framing as too abstract, and redirecting to **real-world cultures everyone recognizes** (Spartans, Aztec, Greece) with **legal historical figures** as commanders. PM also asked to defer AGES from the match layer entirely, and to use the AskUserQuestion tool (ABCD + text-fill) for click-responses instead of prose-only design work.

The session then ran a guided ratification cycle using AskUserQuestion across ~18 questions, landing in order: frame → civs → commanders → tower shape → tower roster → unit roster → kit structure → merge rules → commander abilities (through three naming iterations) → match arc → economy → T4 scope → mythic ladder (T4/T5/T6 proposed, rejected in favor of fusion-as-endgame) → Divinity economy → T4 Demigod rosters → God recipes.

All major design choices landed on the PM-recommended option (except where PM explicitly redirected — abilities, T4 scope, ladder shape). This decision consolidates those ratifications into one filed entry.

## Alternatives considered (at frame level)

- **Option A — Synthetic mythic-history braid (the 2026-04-24 reopen's direction).** Philosophy + real history + comparative religion + mythology braided into a single invented world. Filed as draft in [`2026-04-25-q2-world-pitch-draft.md`](2026-04-25-q2-world-pitch-draft.md). Rejected in-session as too abstract for a TD audience and too far from high-school-recognizable reference points.
- **Option B — Fantasy-original world (Tier Wars typical).** Custom civs, invented mythology, no real-world anchors. Rejected: loses the "everyone knows what a Pyramid is" marketability floor.
- **Option C — Real-cultures + native myth (chosen).** Greek / Aztec / Norse, real commanders, real tower-archetypes on the wrapper, native myth inside. High-school recognizable. Marketable. Silhouette-forward. PM-directed.
- **Option D — Era-ladder (bronze → iron → classical → etc.).** Explicitly rejected by 2026-04-24 reopen as "remix" direction. Not revisited.
- **Option E — Place-first (a specific geography).** Would have forced geographic commitments too early; rejected.

At the structural level, Options within C were presented turn-by-turn via AskUserQuestion (civ count, commander count, tower-shape-shared-vs-civ-specific, tier-count, economy currency-count, fusion-shape, mythic-ladder-depth). PM-recommended option selected at each gate, with three explicit redirects (commander abilities high-school-recognizability, T4 all-6-not-3, fusion-not-standalone-T5-T6).

## Reason chosen + 3x debug loop

**Reason chosen (frame):** Real cultures + native myth gives the game a high-school-recognizable floor that the synthetic braid could never deliver inside a TD-paced gameplay loop. The marketing surface ("Spartans vs. Aztecs vs. Vikings, with gods") is legible at a glance; the myth layer inside the structure preserves design depth. The 2026-04-24 reopen's structural guardrails (mechanics, locks, age-deferral, prototype-freeze) survive the direction-change intact.

### Loop 1 — aggressive critique

1. **"Spartans vs. Aztecs vs. Norse" is the most-done matchup in strategy games.** Age of Empires, Civilization, Total War, Rise of Nations, smaller indie titles. Is this differentiated?
2. **Historical-commander pipeline has real legal footing but cultural-sensitivity risk.** Montezuma II is a flashpoint for Indigenous representation. Leonidas is heavily weighted by 300's aesthetics and right-wing appropriation. Ragnar Lothbrok is fictionalized-as-historical (Vikings TV) and actual Norse scholarship disputes his existence.
3. **T4 Demigods roster is Greek-heavy, Aztec-thin, Norse-TV-popular.** Greek gets Achilles/Hercules/Perseus/Odysseus — all ironclad. Aztec gets "High Priest-King" and "Blood Priest" (archetypal, not named). Norse gets Björn Ironside and Lagertha (recognizable only because of the Vikings TV show). Roster-quality is uneven.
4. **Divinity economy is fragile.** 6 tokens per match, 2 on unlock, 1 per fusion = the entire mythic layer hinges on surviving to round 15+ and managing one resource correctly. One mistake nukes the endgame.
5. **Fusion-as-endgame collapses a lot of content.** Earlier session proposed 54 mythic variants (T4/T5/T6 × 6 × 3). Fusion-only collapses to 18 T4 + 9 Gods = 27 mythic units. Content-density at endgame might feel thin.
6. **"Mundane outside, myth inside" is a strong marketing claim but doesn't really hold.** Trireme Dock, Feathered Serpent Temple, Berserker Lodge are already mythically loaded on the label. The rule is aspirational, not mechanical.
7. **High-school-recognizable floor rules out a lot of good content.** Lagertha is borderline. Nanahuatzin is not recognizable. Harald Bluetooth is recognizable only because of the tech-brand coincidence. Applying the rule strictly narrows the roster painfully for non-Greek civs.
8. **3 civs × 1 commander per civ at launch is thin.** Tower Wars players expect roster breadth at launch. Patch-1 commander per civ softens but doesn't fix.
9. **Fused towers consume both source demigods = a merge penalty at endgame.** The fusion is fun the first time, then feels like a tax.
10. **Three 2026-04-20 decisions still un-rebased.** This ratification re-grounds the theme but doesn't touch the identity-floor / age-history-flavor / commander-pick-upgrade rebase debt.

### Loop 2 — steelman

- **(1) done-before charge:** The differentiator is the **Divinity+Fusion endgame**, not the civ lineup. Age/Civ/RoN don't have tower-merge into gods as a core endgame. Combined with within-civ tower-merge and ability-based commander kits, the real-cultures frame is the *wrapper*, not the *pitch*. It's a marketing-floor choice, not a gameplay-innovation claim.
- **(2) cultural-sensitivity charge:** Real and important. Follow-up #5 tracks it explicitly. No commander roster, unit name, or visual direction should ship without a cultural sensitivity pass. Montezuma II representation specifically needs external input. Leonidas framing should avoid 300's ideological baggage. Ragnar framing should lean legendary (Vikings TV-aware) rather than strict history.
- **(3) uneven-roster charge:** Real. Steelmanned fix: the Aztec T4 Demigod roster deliberately leans **archetypal** (High Priest-King / Jaguar Champion / Blood Priest) because named Aztec demigods are genuinely obscure at HS-recognizable level. This is **not** a bug — it's honest engineering around the source material. Mythical representation is civ-dependent and uneven; forcing Aztec into a named-heroes mold would either misuse the material or invent demigods, both worse than accepting the asymmetry.
- **(4) fragile-economy charge:** Partly real. Divinity is tight but by design — fusion is endgame, not always-on. The 2 mini-boss + 2 main-boss slots after unlock gives 4 fusions theoretical max and 2-3 practical, which aligns with the 3-god roster. Stop-gap: if balance testing shows fusion is unreachable in a meaningful percentage of matches, introduce a Divinity-carryover or one-time-start-match Divinity bonus.
- **(5) thin-endgame charge:** Partly real. 27 mythic units vs. 54 is genuinely less content. But: (a) 9 Gods with fixed recipes are each distinct and replayable, (b) attack-type fusion adds combinatorial depth (Follow-up #2), (c) 2-dev-team shippability dictates lean over maximalist, (d) patch-content can add gods later without disrupting the structure.
- **(6) aspirational-rule charge:** Real. "Mundane outside, myth inside" is a *design heuristic*, not an ironclad invariant. Some names (Trireme Dock / Feathered Serpent Temple) do break it. The rule is directional, not absolute.
- **(7) HS-floor narrows roster charge:** Real and accepted. The rule was an explicit PM direction. Narrowness is the cost of legibility. Roster can widen over time via popular-cultural validation (e.g., a new Viking TV series may make Egil Skallagrímsson HS-recognizable in five years).
- **(8) thin-launch charge:** Real. 3+3 commanders by first patch is the minimum shippable. Two-dev-team dictates lean. Launch roster can grow post-launch without structural change.
- **(9) merge-penalty-at-endgame charge:** Steelmanned fix — the fused God is *substantially stronger* than either source T4. Fusion is a deliberate escalation moment, not a trade. Players willingly consume the sources because the God is the reward. If balance testing shows sources feel "wasted," Gods get buffed.
- **(10) rebase-debt charge:** True. This decision does not resolve the three un-rebased 2026-04-20 decisions. They remain tracked in `PROGRESS.md` Debts Carried and in 2026-04-24 reopen Follow-up #7. Phase 1 exit is still where they reconcile.

### Loop 3 — synthesis

The ratified direction is **structurally sound** provided:

- **(a)** Cultural-sensitivity pass runs before any commander, unit, or visual direction locks (Follow-up #5).
- **(b)** Attack-type mapping lands in the next design cluster (Follow-up #2), or Fusion's endgame distinctness collapses.
- **(c)** "Mundane outside, myth inside" is held as a *heuristic*, not an invariant.
- **(d)** Asymmetric mythic-roster quality across civs is accepted, not papered over — PM chose HS-recognizability as the floor, and Aztec-thin is the honest consequence.
- **(e)** Divinity economy is stress-tested in playtest to confirm fusion is reachable in typical matches, with carryover stop-gap ready if not.
- **(f)** The real-cultures frame is held as a *marketing floor + design wrapper*, not a differentiator claim. The differentiator is the Divinity-Fusion endgame.

Actionable direction: ratify this consolidation now; file Follow-ups #1-#10 as the ongoing work queue; cascade the frame change into CONCEPT.md + stage docs + README in subsequent turns (not this turn). Continue with attack-type mapping next.
