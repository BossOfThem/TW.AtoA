## Decision

Phase 1 is reopened in full. The 2026-04-21 ratification — 3/3/3 shape (3 lineages Ash/Nature/Prayer, 3 tiers Dust/Form/Apotheosis, 3 commanders), dungeon cosmology, and Ash-enabler hybrid model, Reversibility Hard — is **superseded** at the source. The Step A cascade execution that implemented 2026-04-21 across `concept/phase-1..7.md`, the `CONCEPT.md` hub, `README.md` critical-context #3, and stub-amends at `stages/01/04/05/06` is **not force-reverted**; those §-edits remain in the repo as the living record of the superseded direction and as raw material for the reopen, but they are no longer Accepted as Phase 1 truth. The 2026-04-22 Step B prototype-reshape plan (Proposed) is **superseded**: its 18-sub-step data/scene migration to 3/3/3 is void. The thematic direction for the reopen is a **synthetic mythic-history braid** — a single invented world whose raw material is drawn jointly from real-world history, comparative religion, mythology, and philosophy, braided into its own cosmology rather than assembled as pastiche. The age model (within-match vs. seasonal vs. campaign-chapters) is **deferred** until world, story, and background are established. Mechanics (TD / merge / debuff / magic) remain fixed. §2.4a accessibility floor [LOCKED] and §5.4 naming conventions [LOCKED] remain untouched. Prototype is frozen against concept edits until Phase 1 exits; the Step 5 playtest gate and any prototype reshape are downstream of the reopen, not priorities.

## Context

Two pressures converged on 2026-04-24. First, the PM read the 2026-04-21 Step A cascade end-to-end — the §-rewrites that collapsed 5/11/5 to 3/3/3, introduced Ash/Nature/Prayer lineages as prose placeholders, named Dust/Form/Apotheosis tiers, and locked the "Ash → Altar" arc as load-bearing — and judged that the world/theme layer underneath those structural edits was still under-specified. 2026-04-21's own Loop 1 had flagged "dungeon cosmology is evocative but structureless" and deferred tier-gameplay identity to later A4/A5 work; that debt was never paid, and the 2026-04-22 Step B plan proceeded to data-shape migration without ever closing the cosmology question. Second, the thematic ambition widened. Rather than a single inspirational spine ("Ash to Altar" ritual arc), the PM wants a world whose texture reads as the product of historical, religious, mythological, and philosophical sediment at once. That ambition does not fit inside 2026-04-21's framing without silent edits to Phase 1, which CLAUDE.md forbids. The correct move is a proper reopen, filed as this decision, with 2026-04-21 formally superseded rather than silently walked back.

2026-04-21's Reversibility-Hard label was earned precisely to force this pathway. Its Reversibility note states: *"Reversal is permitted but must be staged as an explicit upstream reopen per `CLAUDE.md` cascade discipline, not papered over."* This entry is that upstream reopen.

## Alternatives considered

- **Option A — Narrow reopen (theme only).** Keep the 3/3/3 shape, the Dust/Form/Apotheosis tier-naming convention, and the Ash-enabler hybrid topology; reopen only the thematic surface. Rejected: "synthetic mythic-history braid" is not a surface reskin. It reshapes cosmology, which reshapes what tiers are (transitions through what? ritual stages? historical eras? ontological registers?), which reshapes whether Ash-enabler is still the right hybrid topology (it was justified under dungeon cosmology as a legibility spine; the new frame may want any-pair hybrids or no hybrid-topology distinction at all). Holding shape and topology frozen while theme rewrites underneath them would force exactly the silent upstream edit the cascade forbids.
- **Option B — Full reopen including mechanics.** Reopen Phase 1 *and* the TD/merge/debuff/magic mechanics contract. Rejected: mechanics are the most externally stable part of the concept — they survived two playtests, the 2026-04-20 DNA pass, and the 2026-04-21 Step A rewrite without structural challenge. Reopening them would discard stable ground to solve a problem that lives upstream. Mechanics are the constraint the world must fit, not the variable.
- **Option C — Full Phase 1 reopen; mechanics + [LOCKED] items preserved; age model deferred; 2026-04-21 formally superseded; 2026-04-22 Step B superseded; Step A cascade edits preserved in-tree as superseded-record.** (Chosen.)

## Reason chosen

Option C is the only choice that honors cascade discipline (no silent edits, no force-reverts that destroy the superseded record), preserves earned ground (mechanics, locked items), names concrete cascade consequences (2026-04-22 Step B voided; Step A §-edits demoted from Accepted to superseded-record), and refuses premature commitment (age model).

**3x debug loop.**

*Loop 1 — aggressive critique.* A full Phase 1 reopen is expensive in real, enumerable terms. The 2026-04-21 Step A cascade wrote concrete content to `concept/phase-1.md §1.1` (Ash→Altar arc), `phase-3.md §3.2/§3.3/§3.5/§3.8` (3-lineage/3-tier/whiteboard-exit), `phase-4.md §4.1/§4.2/§4.3/§4.5/§4.6/§4.7` (roster 5→3, divergence 2-fork OPEN, Ash-enabler hybrids, tar-pit→tier-gate, currency OPEN, enemy direction OPEN), `phase-5.md §5.1/§5.2/§5.3` (MVP=3/3/3, build phases, silhouette-forward mythic), `phase-6.md §6.1–§6.4` (tier-persistence, Post-hybrid-families milestone), `phase-7.md §7.1/§7.3/§7.4` (roster closed at 3), plus the `CONCEPT.md` hub vision line, `README.md` critical-context #3, and four stub-amends at `stages/01/04/05/06`. That is at least 18 distinct §-edits the reopen demotes from Accepted to superseded-record — every one becomes a candidate for re-ratification when Phase 1 exits. The 2026-04-22 Step B plan (18 sub-steps) is now void before a single sub-step landed; the planning effort is not recoverable. The three 2026-04-20 decisions that 2026-04-21 *rebased* (commander-identity-floor, age-history-flavor-mapmods, commander-pick-identity-upgrade) are now un-rebased — their rebase banners point at a superseded parent, and their Status field needs reconciliation. "Synthetic mythic-history braid" is also dangerously underconstrained: four source domains multiplied together produce a nearly unbounded design space, and without a sharper rubric the reopen can drift across sessions. Deferring the age model risks permanent deferral — age/tier mechanics are the pivot the prototype turns on, and if the world never decides, the age model never decides, and prototype progress stalls indefinitely. The working title "Ash to Altar" is also now thematically disconnected from the intended direction and will leak into every scratch artifact until it is replaced. Finally, Reversibility Hard is correctly labeled but under-costed: if the reopen lands a world direction that later needs to reverse, every 2026-04-20 mechanical decision that name-drops commanders, ages, or biomes will need another rebase-or-supersede pass.

*Loop 2 — steelman.* Loop 1 overweights sunk cost. The 18 Step A §-edits were written against a world-frame (dungeon cosmology) that had explicitly unpaid debt — 2026-04-21's own Loop 3 admitted tier-gameplay identity was still owed to A4/A5. The §-edits are not load-bearing prose, they are structural placeholders; rewriting them under a new frame will preserve the structural moves (e.g., tier-persistence over age-persistence is a *shape* claim about meta-progression, and it may well survive the reopen even if the tiers get re-named and re-numbered). The 2026-04-20 mechanical decisions rebased by 2026-04-21 (identity-floor structure, age-gate *logic*, silhouette-test+XP-ladder logic) are mechanical, not thematic — their surviving clauses stay Accepted; only their thematic content was ever at risk. The 2026-04-22 Step B plan's 18-sub-step loss is real but small — it was Proposed, not executed; zero sub-steps landed; the planning exercise itself is reusable as a template for whatever prototype reshape the new Phase 1 implies. Loop 1's "unbounded design space" worry is answered by the rubric: *synthetic* rules out remix; *braided* rules out side-by-side pastiche; *one invented world* rules out multiverse; §5.4 naming conventions [LOCKED] constrain the lexicon. Those four constraints collapse the space fast. On the deferral of the age model: deferral is explicitly conditional (world + story + background established), which is a reachable milestone, not a moving one, and the exit rubric below names it. On the title: "Ash to Altar" working-title status is explicit in §5.4 discipline; rewriting will be cheap because nothing in the repo depends on the string. On Reversibility Hard: the label is the protection — it raises the bar for any future sub-decision to contradict the reopen, which is exactly the cascade hygiene a Phase 1 change deserves.

*Loop 3 — synthesis.* Ratify the full reopen, but ship it with four stop-gaps against Loop 1's real concerns. (1) **Scope fence:** this decision reopens Phase 1 *only*; downstream phases re-evaluate only when Phase 1 exits and names a constraint they violate. 2026-04-20 *mechanical* decisions stay Accepted. 2026-04-20 *thematic* `[PROPOSAL]` content expects rewrite. Step A §-edits stay in-tree as superseded-record, not force-reverted. (2) **Completion rubric:** Phase 1 exits when the PM can answer, in one page, *what the world is*, *what the Commander's role in it is*, and *what hybrid topology expresses about the world* — these three answers together gate age-model reopening and title-lock reopening. (3) **Pacing discipline:** Socratic question-per-turn cadence per HANDOFF's Q1..Q10 sequence, PM drives, no batching into an omnibus Phase 1 rewrite. (4) **Bookkeeping fence:** the status of 2026-04-21, the status of 2026-04-22 Step B plan, and the rebase relationships of the three 2026-04-20 entries rebased by 2026-04-21 are each reconciled in their own follow-up turns (one file per turn) — not silently left as dangling references to a superseded parent.

Option C with those four stop-gaps is the choice.
