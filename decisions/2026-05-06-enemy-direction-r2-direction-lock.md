## Decision

**Option C ratified.** §4.7 enemy direction locks to **civ-matched boss-tier enemies + shared cross-cultural regular-wave pool**. The boss-tier cadence (mini-bosses at R5/R15/R25; main bosses at R10/R20/R30) is **civ-matched per the 2026-04-27 historic-arc table verbatim**: Greek main-boss arc = Persian-Wars-mortal (Xerxes I at R30 + Persian Immortal vanguard at R25-29); Aztec main-boss arc = mortal-to-mythic two-phase (Tlaxcalan war-leader → Tezcatlipoca avatar at R30); Norse main-boss arc = pure-myth (Jörmungandr Colossal+Spirit breach at R30 + tidal-omen kraken-tier Beast+Spirit minions at R25-29). The 24 regular-wave rounds (R1-R4 / R6-R9 / R11-R14 / R16-R19 / R21-R24 / R26-R29) draw from a **shared cross-cultural regular-wave pool** — one bestiary referenced at gameplay speed via the 5-armor-tag silhouette + icon (no civ-flavor surface differences in regular-wave creep silhouettes at launch).

**Boss-tier civ-distinct armor-class clustering** (binding the per-civ RN signature_creep_types verbatim):

| Civ | Boss-tier armor-class cluster | R30 armor tags (locked content-skeleton) | Mini-boss armor-class space |
|-----|-------------------------------|------------------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Greek | **Colossal + Shielded** | Xerxes I = Shielded (heavy royal armor) + Persian Immortal vanguard R25-29 = Shielded swarm-density | R5/R15/R25 mini-bosses pull from Shielded (hoplite-rivals → satrap commanders → Persian Immortal champion) — one mini-boss per cadence may add Colossal scale (satrap-tier or champion-tier elephant-of-war analogue) |
| Aztec | **Beast + Spirit + Unarmored** | Tlaxcalan phase-1 = Unarmored + Beast (jaguar/eagle warrior framing within mortal scale); Tezcatlipoca phase-2 = Spirit (avatar form) | R5/R15/R25 mini-bosses pull from Beast + Spirit + Unarmored (rival altepetl warlords → Cipactli-tier myth fauna → priest-king of Triple Alliance enemy state) — Beast at R15 (myth-fauna tier) is the densest |
| Norse | **Shielded + Unarmored** at sub-boss; **Colossal + Spirit** at R30 | Jörmungandr = Colossal + Spirit (Eddic cosmic scale); R25-29 vanguard = Beast + Spirit (kraken-tier minions per 2026-04-27 verbatim) | R5/R15/R25 mini-bosses pull from Shielded + Unarmored at the lower tiers (rival jarls → Draugr lord → Jötunn herald); R25 Jötunn herald may add Colossal scale at half-Jörmungandr-magnitude |

The cluster respects the per-civ RN matchup_affinity matrix (Greek STRONG vs Colossal+Shielded → Greek's own boss-tier clusters in those classes is the design's intentional self-affinity surface — players running Greek face a *boss-tier difficulty curve their civ is built to answer*; same self-affinity logic for Aztec STRONG vs Beast+Spirit+Unarmored and Norse STRONG vs Shielded+Unarmored). The Norse R30 Colossal+Spirit boss is *not* a Norse-civ self-affinity case — it is the cosmic-scale antagonist *Norse is intentionally weak against* (Norse WEAK vs Spirit per RN), creating the Eddic Ragnarök-foreshadow tension at R30 where the player has to assemble cross-civ cooperation (Foresight-coin / patch-1 commanders / multi-civ play hook) or burn the Divinity stack at peak. This is the design's first explicit **boss-tier-as-civ-WEAK falsification surface** — locked here because it directly anchors Norse's identity_hook narrative rather than violating it.

**Shared regular-wave pool armor-tag distribution targets (24 regular-wave rounds, 5-armor-class grain, civ-neutral):**

| Armor class | Target distribution (% of regular-wave creep instances across R1-R29 non-boss waves) | Rationale |
|-------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-----------|
| **Unarmored** | **35-45%** | Common swarm baseline; legible at gameplay speed; populated by all 3 civs' Unarmored-affinity towers |
| **Shielded** | **20-30%** | Heavy elite mid-game pressure; populated by Crushing/Piercing-affinity towers |
| **Beast** | **15-20%** | Myth-monster mid-game pressure; populated by Slashing/Fire-affinity towers (Aztec self-affinity) |
| **Spirit** | **8-12%** | Lower-density to preserve Spirit as a *threat-class*, not a baseline; populated by Arcane/Divine-affinity towers |
| **Colossal** | **3-7%** | Reserved for elite/colossal-elite waves only; very low density at regular-wave grain so boss-tier Colossal stays special |

Targets are **distribution bands**, not exact per-wave allocations — R3 R11 variance grammar will author the random-seeded armor-tag mix per wave that *samples* these targets within difficulty-appropriate distributions. Bands defend against three structural matchup-failure modes:

1. **Unwinnable structural matchup avoidance.** Per-tower RN type-coverage table binds: Greek lacks Poison, Aztec lacks Crushing, Norse lacks Poison + Divine T1-T3. Regular-wave pool **never presents an armor-class whose only-effective-counter-attack-types are absent from the player's civ-locked roster.** No Spirit-armor wave demands Divine-only coverage from a Norse T1-T3 player; no Shielded-armor wave demands Crushing-only coverage from an Aztec player. Counter-attack-type availability per civ at the 5-armor grain is verified at RN cross-arc audit (item iii). The bands above are upper-bounded against this constraint at every civ's worst-case tower selection.
2. **Per-civ matchup_affinity surface coherence.** The 5×3 civ-aggregate matchup matrix is non-uniform: Greek WEAK vs Beast / Aztec WEAK vs Shielded / Norse WEAK vs Spirit + Divine-pre-T4. Regular-wave pool distribution must *make each civ's WEAK class encounterable enough that the WEAK affinity is a felt identity surface* — not a vestigial label. Beast at 15-20% guarantees Greek players encounter their WEAK class meaningfully; Shielded at 20-30% guarantees Aztec players encounter theirs; Spirit at 8-12% guarantees Norse players encounter theirs (boss-tier Spirit at R30 Jörmungandr completes the surface for Norse).
3. **Three-civ-three-equation-form fingerprint preservation.** Greek deceleration-weighted equation needs sufficient **target-time-integral surface** to mass-process at lockdown grain — Unarmored swarm density 35-45% provides that. Aztec kill-multiplication-weighted equation needs **high-target-count + Poison-stack-saturation surface** — Beast + Unarmored aggregate 50-65% provides high target count, Aztec self-affinity stacks compounding procs across the saturation. Norse summon-cleave-propagation-weighted equation needs **tight-formation high-target-count + Bleed-density-cascade surface** — Shielded + Unarmored aggregate 55-75% provides the tight-formation cell-count for cleave-propagation peak. All three equation forms find their pressure-surface inside the same shared distribution; pressures uniformly favoring one equation form would violate the fingerprint and reopen this round.

**Civ-neutral effect-type language locked.** Regular-wave creep references the 5-armor taxonomy (Unarmored / Shielded / Beast / Spirit / Colossal) verbatim per 2026-04-26 attack-type lock. **No creature-name authoring** beyond the locked content-skeleton boss roster (Xerxes / Tlaxcalan / Tezcatlipoca / Jörmungandr verbatim). **No art / VFX / civ-flavor surface direction prose** anywhere in this decision — Cultural-sensitivity Follow-up #5 hard-gate scheduled at Phase-4 exit per 2026-05-06 post-arc ratification item 4. Mini-boss candidate names from 2026-04-27 (hoplite-rivals → satrap commanders → Persian Immortal champion / rival altepetl warlords → Cipactli-tier myth fauna → priest-king of Triple-Alliance enemy state / rival jarls → Draugr lord → Jötunn herald) remain placeholder-pending-Follow-up-#5 — armor-class clustering above binds the *armor-tag space* the eventual roster must populate; specific creature names lock at Phase 5 under Follow-up #5 consultation.

## Reason chosen

Option C is the **only direction compatible with all six R1-scope load-bearing surfaces simultaneously**, with three reinforcing reasons:

1. **All three evidentiary streams converge.** §4.7 leading placeholder is C; 2026-04-27 historic-arc table is C-shaped at boss tier verbatim; per-civ RN signature_creep_types are civ-distinct boss-tier armor-class clusters. Forking to A or B requires explicit supersession decision against multiple upstream locks (A: 2026-04-27 + Follow-up #11; B: lean-launch floor + Follow-up #5 hard-gate). C requires zero upstream supersessions. **C is the only direction the cascade discipline allows without reopening upstream phases.**
2. **Cultural-sensitivity Follow-up #5 hard-gate compatible.** C confines civ-matched art surface to **boss-tier only** (R5/R10/R15/R20/R25/R30 — 6 boss instances per civ across the match). Follow-up #5 review at Phase-4 exit reviews 18 boss-instance art-locks (6 per civ × 3 civs), not the 100+ regular-wave creep silhouettes that B would require. Three-times burden becomes one-time + 6× per civ — manageable within Follow-up #5's scheduling envelope. The shared regular-wave pool stays civ-neutral: silhouettes + 5-armor-tag icons read at gameplay speed without per-civ flavor authoring during this arc.
3. **Lean-launch floor + three-civ-three-equation-form fingerprint preserved.** Lean-launch floor (3 civs / 18 T1-T3 / 15 units / 18 T4 / 9 Gods) implies **single regular-wave bestiary** — C delivers exactly this. The fingerprint (Greek deceleration-weighted / Aztec kill-multiplication-weighted / Norse summon-cleave-propagation-weighted) finds its pressure-surface inside one shared distribution at the bands above; pressures map cleanly to all three equation forms simultaneously without favoring one. **C is the only direction that keeps the fingerprint a *spec-level invariant* rather than a per-civ-pool curiosity.**

### 3x debug loop on Direction C ratification

**Loop 1 — aggressive critique.** Option C's "civ-matched bosses + shared regular-wave pool" hybrid has a structural seam at the boss/regular-wave boundary that risks reader-experience incoherence. A player playing Aztec faces *shared cross-cultural regular-wave creeps* for 24 of 30 rounds, then faces *civ-matched Tlaxcalan/Tezcatlipoca bosses* at 6 of 30. The narrative framing — "this is your commander's compressed historic-and-mythic arc" — depends on the boss-tier civ-matching to land the cross-civ tonal escalation, but 80% of the player's time is spent fighting creeps that don't reflect that arc. Furthermore, the 2026-04-27 historic-arc table specifies *boss-fight-mechanic* civ-matching at boss tier — but the *cross-civ tonal escalation* (mortal → bridge → mythic per civ) traverses the *whole 30-round arc*, including regular waves. A purely shared regular-wave pool flattens the tonal-escalation beat at the regular-wave grain — Greek waves R1-R29 read identically to Norse waves R1-R29, undermining the "Greek run reads as recorded history; Norse run is myth from minute one" framing locked at 2026-04-27. The boss-vs-regular seam is *the* structural risk of Option C — the design pretends each civ's run feels distinct, but mechanically only the 6 boss instances differ.

Furthermore, the Norse-WEAK-vs-Spirit boss-tier-as-civ-WEAK falsification surface (Jörmungandr Colossal+Spirit at R30 against Norse Spirit-WEAK affinity) is a single-instance design move at one boss in one civ. If the design intent is "boss-tier as civ-WEAK explicit narrative tension" generally, the table doesn't deliver — Greek's R30 Xerxes is Shielded (Greek STRONG vs Shielded → Greek faces a *self-affinity* boss, not a tension boss). The boss-tier-as-civ-WEAK move is asymmetric across the three civs, which either is intentional design subtlety or a design hole hand-waved by the table.

Finally, the shared regular-wave pool's distribution bands (Unarmored 35-45% / Shielded 20-30% / Beast 15-20% / Spirit 8-12% / Colossal 3-7%) sum to 81-114% — the upper-band-stacked case overshoots 100% and the lower-band-stacked case undershoots 81%. The bands are not internally consistent as a probability distribution. R3 R11 grammar will need to author tighter constraints (e.g., conditional sampling: weight Beast higher when Greek is locked civ to surface Greek-WEAK affinity meaningfully; weight Spirit higher when Norse is locked civ; etc.) but R2 leaves the constraint unstated and trusts R3 to recover it.

**Loop 2 — steelman.** The boss-vs-regular-wave-pool seam (Loop-1 critique #1) is a *feature*, not a bug. The 2026-04-27 historic-arc table explicitly framed boss-tier as the dramatized civ-matched surface and regular waves as the chassis — "same 30-round chassis; different narrative scale per civ" — exactly the boss-tier civ-matching + regular-wave shared chassis Option C implements. Tonal escalation at the regular-wave grain is delivered through the *boss-tier cadence rhythm* (every 5 rounds, the dramatized civ-matched antagonist surface re-asserts) rather than per-creep silhouettes; the player's mental model of "this is my Greek commander's run against the Persian Wars" is anchored every 5 rounds at boss-tier and the regular waves between are tactical chassis, not narrative substance. This is the same chassis-vs-narrative split at 2026-04-27.

The boss-tier-as-civ-WEAK asymmetry (Loop-1 critique #2) is intentionally asymmetric per the 2026-04-27 "mixed historicity is intentional" framing. Greek R30 Xerxes is Shielded (Greek STRONG): Leonidas's apocalypse is *facing the recorded historical foe his civ is built to answer at the right scale* — historical-narrative tension. Norse R30 Jörmungandr is Colossal+Spirit (Norse WEAK vs Spirit): Ragnar's apocalypse is *facing the cosmic-myth foe Norse cannot answer at the right scale alone* — Ragnarök-narrative tension forcing cross-civ play (multi_civ_play_hook). Aztec R30 Tezcatlipoca is two-phase Unarmored→Spirit (Aztec STRONG vs both): Montezuma's apocalypse is *facing the foe his civ is built for, but at scales his civ struggles with at boss-tier-armor-class-density* — sacrificial-narrative tension. Three different narrative-tension shapes by civ, all anchored at one boss instance per civ. Asymmetry is the design intent.

The distribution-band internal-inconsistency critique (Loop-1 critique #3) lands cleanly on the bands as authored — but the constraint R2 is binding is *upper-and-lower bounds the regular-wave-pool sample-distribution must respect at the 5-class grain*, not a single sealed probability distribution. R3 R11 variance grammar's job is to author the random-seeded armor-tag mix per wave that *samples within the bands* under conditional difficulty modifiers (per-mode (g)/(p)/(q) tuning + locked-civ matchup-affinity surface adjustments). The bands are *constraint envelopes*, not the distribution itself. The 81-114% slack is *intentional design space for R3 to land within*. R2 binding the sealed distribution would force-fit the design space and deny R3 its falsification gate.

**Loop 3 — synthesis.** Option C is correct, with the boss-vs-regular seam, asymmetric WEAK-tension, and band-slack all reframed as Loop-1-mistakenly-flagged-as-bugs that Loop-2 reframed as design intent. No mid-arc corrections needed at R2 — but **two cross-property coherence hooks to R3** are added explicitly:

1. **R3 must author conditional sampling** within the regular-wave pool bands. Per-civ matchup_affinity surface coherence (per-civ RN matrix) requires Beast weighting up for Greek-locked civ at regular-wave grain (Greek-WEAK encounterable) / Shielded weighting up for Aztec-locked civ (Aztec-WEAK encounterable) / Spirit weighting up for Norse-locked civ (Norse-WEAK encounterable). R3 binds the conditional-sampling weights at the variance-grammar layer; R2's bands are the envelope.
2. **R3 must defend the band-slack against unwinnable structural matchups.** The 81-114% sum-slack means R3's sampling can drift into edge cases where one wave hits 100% Spirit (Norse player + WEAK + lacks Divine T1-T3 = unwinnable). R3 R11 grammar must include **per-wave armor-class diversity constraint** (e.g., minimum 3 of 5 classes per wave; max-single-class cap per wave) preventing degenerate single-class waves under any seed.

R3 cross-property coherence hook follows.
