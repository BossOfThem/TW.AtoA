## Decision

Bind seven mechanical fields across the **15 Greek launch towers** (6 T1-T3 mainline + 6 T4 Demigod + 3 God) per the locked 7-field schema from R1 scope. Per-tower magnitudes within ±20% of the §4.11.5 tier-archetype DPS ladder; cd / range within tier-archetype bands; `attack_type` verbatim-confirms 2026-04-26 mapping; `status_proc` inherits the 2026-04-26 status-proc table with magnitude/duration authored per tier-intensity (T1-T3 baseline → T4 ~1.4× intensity → God paired alternation). All 15 rows carry `controlled_by: leonidas` affinity-target tag — Greek = Control lane lock per per-commander R1-R5 (Hard reversibility). `aux_slot_compat` defaults to §4.6a universal across all 15 rows; no civ-identity hook narrows the catalog at this round (universal compat preserves cross-civ aux-build flexibility).

**Civ-identity hook (Greek = lockdown / control archetype):** Greek roster status-procs concentrate on **kinetic suppression** — armor-shred (Piercing × 2 sources: Phalanx + Trireme Dock), stagger (Crushing × 1: Acropolis), hex attack/cast-speed slow (Arcane × 1: Oracle), splash burn (Fire × 1: Colossus), and Divine smite (Divine × 1: Parthenon). Greek covers 5 of 7 attack types; missing Slashing and Poison among T1-T3 (Slashing arrives at T4 via Theseus; Poison stays absent at launch — multi-civ play hook). The lockdown archetype reads through the **temporal compounding** of these procs: Piercing armor-shred refreshes faster than Crushing stagger refreshes, which refreshes faster than Arcane hex, which refreshes faster than Fire burn DoT — so a clustered Greek build inside Leonidas's Spartan-Training 2-cell aura layers four refresh-cycles on a single moving enemy and approaches an effective stop-state by the time the enemy enters Last-Stand zone-denial. This is the lockdown signature: "many small slows, layered, on top of one big stun." `cd` lands moderate (workhorse cadence 1.0–1.6s for mainline / 1.8–2.6s for siege variants); `range` lands moderate (3–5 cells with Acropolis siege at 5 / Trireme Dock fast-fire at 3.5). `dps` clusters at-or-slightly-above tier baseline for kinetic-type towers (Phalanx, Trireme Dock at 110% baseline) and at-or-slightly-below for utility-type towers (Acropolis at 90%, Oracle at 100%, Colossus at 100%, Parthenon at 100%) — the variance direction expresses control-archetype's preference for sustained pressure over peak burst.

**Per-commander affinity-target tag (`controlled_by: leonidas`).** All 15 Greek rows tag the receiving side of Leonidas's three-ability kit:
- **Spartan Training** (passive): every Greek-civ tower within 2-cell hex-distance of the anointed Greek tower receives the +15% slow-aura. Tag is symmetric — the tag binds the target-side of a civ-matched-tower-aura interface.
- **This Is Sparta!** (active, 30s CD): cast at player-aimed cell, 4s hard-stun + 1-cell knockback in 3-cell radius. Tower-side hook is **none directly** — towers within radius continue firing at stunned enemies (which now sit still, easier to hit). The `controlled_by` tag denotes towers that benefit from the trapped-enemy state; all Greek towers benefit.
- **The Last Stand** (signature, once-per-match free): 12s persistent zone-denial in 4-cell radius. Same as This Is Sparta! pattern — towers within zone benefit from the dead-zone full-stun. The tag covers this.

The tag does NOT cross civ lanes — no Aztec or Norse tower carries `controlled_by: leonidas` (would be a cascade violation against the per-commander R1-R5 Hard-reversibility lane locks).

## Per-tier reading

**T1 paragraph.** The Greek T1 floor reads as a coverage roster: every primary `attack_type` Greek covers (Piercing × 2, Crushing × 1, Arcane × 1, Fire × 1, Divine × 1) is available at base T1 cost (100 T) with the locked status-proc kind already firing at base intensity. A new player picking Greek lays Phalanx + Trireme Dock for sustained Piercing pressure, drops an Acropolis for Shielded-armor priority, and starts the Oracle / Colossus / Parthenon decision based on the wave-mix telegraph. cd-spread 0.8s (Trireme Dock) → 2.4s (Acropolis) means the same coverage roster reads differently depending on placement-coverage axis (b): a Trireme Dock at engagement-coefficient ε=0.6 produces ~5.4 cd-cycles per lane traversal, while an Acropolis at the same ε produces ~1.8 cycles — but each Acropolis cycle commits 162 raw T3-equivalent damage with stagger. T1 dps lands clustered at 18–22 per the ±20% band; the "20 dmg/sec floor" reads as honest ground for the rest of the curve.

**T2 paragraph.** Merging two same-civ same-tier T1s produces a T2 at 60 dmg/sec baseline (clustered 54–64 in this roster). The T2 tier is where Greek's lockdown archetype starts paying compounding dividends: a Phalanx + Acropolis pair merged twice (one each way) yields one Phalanx-T2 + one Acropolis-T2, where the Phalanx-T2 (cd 1.2s) refreshes its −1 armor every 3s while the Acropolis-T2 (cd 2.4s) fires its first stagger inside the 3s window — so the next Phalanx-T2 shot lands on a stripped + slowed enemy. Spartan-Training adds 15% slow on top. The matchup-correctness axis (a) — Hard 75% threshold — starts mattering meaningfully here because the T2 dps cluster is tight (±10% off baseline) and per-shot identity (cd / proc) drives outcomes.

**T3 paragraph.** Two T2 same-civ merges yield a T3 at 180 dmg/sec baseline (clustered 162–192 here). T3 is where Hard expert thresholds kick in (placement-coverage 65%, ability-uptime 60% per §4.11.8) and where Leonidas's Last-Stand zone-denial pairs with the Greek T3 cluster for the canonical lockdown read: a Greek T3 cluster of 3–4 towers inside the 4-cell Last-Stand zone holds a 12s window of zero-mobility-enemies eating ~720 dmg/sec from the cluster (4 × 180 dps) plus per-shot status-proc contributions. The cd-band 0.8–2.4s persists; the per-tower variance has translated up the ladder coherently. Fusion-recipe partners are now visible in the roster (Hercules + Jason → Zeus; Theseus + Perseus → Athena; Achilles + Odysseus → Poseidon all latent) — but Fusion still requires reaching T4 first.

**T4 Demigod paragraph.** T4 Demigods land at 540 dmg/sec baseline (clustered 480–600 in this roster). Greek T4 ladder fills two civ-roster type-holes: Theseus brings Slashing (the Greek T1-T3 had no Slashing source) and Hercules brings the Crushing-peak (Acropolis was the only T1-T3 Crushing source, capped at T3=162 dps; Hercules at T4=580 is ~3.5× that). The cd-band slightly shifts: rapid T4 (Achilles 1.0s, Theseus 0.9s, Odysseus 1.4s) vs slow T4 (Perseus 1.6s, Jason 1.8s, Hercules 2.6s). Range-band stays mid (3–5 cells). T4 status-proc intensity bumps (−2 armor, 7% bleed, 30% stagger, 12% smite, 35% hex) mean the Greek lockdown stack at T4 saturates faster — three T4 Demigods inside Spartan-Training aura produce a near-zero-mobility state on Beast / Shielded / Spirit / Colossal armor classes (per the 2026-04-26 RPS matrix). Demigod identity reads through the per-shot character — Achilles' rapid spear strips armor faster than any Greek tower, Theseus' sword bleeds, Hercules' club staggers, Perseus' divine smite reveals Spirit stealth. Three of the six (Hercules, Jason, Achilles, Odysseus) gate the three Greek Fusions.

**God paragraph.** Three Greek Gods land at 1080 dmg/sec baseline (1050–1100 here). Each God carries a 2-type alternating-per-shot proc set per locked 2026-04-26 Fusion model: Zeus stagger × smite, Athena bleed × smite, Poseidon armor-shred × hex. Range-band creeps up (4.5–5.5 cells; Poseidon longest at 5.5). cd-band sits 1.2–2.2s. The peak Greek-lockdown read is **Last-Stand zone overlapping Zeus range** — 12s of 4-cell hard-stun + Zeus alternating Crushing stagger + Divine smite at 1100 dps = ~13,200 raw damage in zone over 12s, plus the Crushing stagger-on-stun-already-stunned redundancy and the Divine smite-bonus on Colossal R30-boss cumulating to ~14,500–15,500 effective damage. Athena fills the Slashing+Divine lane that Theseus bridged at T4. Poseidon's range advantage and dual-proc lockdown profile (armor-shred + hex, no stagger) gives Greek a non-Last-Stand-dependent peak — Poseidon at 5.5-cell range threads two adjacent lanes on Horde-B / PvP-Maze layouts. Three Gods, three lockdown signatures: Zeus = burst-stun-zone, Athena = bleed-Divine, Poseidon = sustained-pressure-corridor.

## Reason chosen

The 15 Greek rows author the Control lane lock per-commander R1-R5 Leonidas spec into the per-tower target-side. Three specific bindings hold:

1. **`controlled_by: leonidas` is uniform across all 15 Greek rows** because Leonidas's Spartan-Training aura applies civ-wide (any Greek-civ tower within 2-cell anointed-tower aura). No per-tower variation in *which* towers receive the aura — only in *how each tower's own `cd` / `range` / `status_proc` interacts* with the aura's 15% slow, the active's 4s hard-stun, and the signature's 12s zone-denial. The interactions are spelled out in each row's `notes`.
2. **dps-band variance direction expresses control-archetype.** Kinetic-type towers (Piercing × 2) sit at +10% baseline because Piercing's armor-shred refresh-rate is the lockdown enabler; utility-type towers (Crushing, Arcane, Fire, Divine) sit at-or-slightly-below baseline because their value comes from the proc kind, not the raw dps. T4 Demigod cluster centers near baseline 540 with Hercules slightly above (peak Crushing) and Odysseus slightly below (peak utility/range). God cluster centers near baseline 1080 with Zeus and Poseidon slightly above (peak burst / peak range respectively) and Athena slightly below (band-middle).
3. **cd / range bands match tier-archetype roles.** Mainline T1-T3 spans rapid-fire (0.8s) to siege (2.4s); T4 narrows to 0.9–2.6s; God settles into 1.2–2.2s. Range bands stay mid (3–5.5 cells) with Poseidon's 5.5-cell peak as the longest in the Greek roster — which gives Greek a non-Last-Stand-dependent corridor-pressure profile distinct from Zeus's burst-zone signature. The lockdown archetype reads through the **temporal compounding** of multiple status-procs at moderate cadence, not through extreme cd or extreme range.

### 3x debug loop on per-tower magnitude variance

**Loop 1 — aggressive critique.** Authoring all 15 rows with the same `controlled_by: leonidas` tag is mechanically vacuous — if every Greek tower carries the same affinity-target tag, the tag isn't doing per-tower work; it's doing per-civ work and the tag is misnamed (should be `civ: greek`, with Leonidas's aura reading off civ at runtime). Authoring 15 rows with cd-band 0.8–2.6s + range-band 3–5.5 is broad enough that the bands aren't actually constraining variance — any reasonable tower could fit. dps-band ±20% allows 22 / 64 / 192 (Phalanx, +10%) and 18 / 54 / 162 (Acropolis, −10%) — but this is internal to the tier-band, not an expression of civ-archetype. Where's the civ-identity hook actually doing per-tower work?

**Loop 2 — steelman.** The `controlled_by: leonidas` tag is correct as authored — it tags the receiving-side of three distinct Leonidas interfaces (Spartan-Training aura / This-Is-Sparta cell-cast / Last-Stand zone-denial), not just the passive aura. The tag's payload is the *interface kind*, not the *per-tower switch*. At runtime, the Greek tower's behavior under each Leonidas ability is determined by the tower's own `cd` / `range` / `status_proc` fields (e.g., a Phalanx inside Last-Stand zone fires every 1.2s on stunned targets; an Acropolis inside the same zone fires every 2.4s on the same targets — different actual contributions). The tag binds the *eligibility* for the interface; the per-tower fields determine the *contribution magnitude*. This is the schema's intended discipline: the tag locks lane-coherence, the fields express per-tower variance.

cd / range bands ARE doing per-tower work via the variance-direction argument: Piercing-type towers get fastest cd (0.8s Trireme Dock, 1.0s Achilles), and the lockdown archetype is *defined* by sustained kinetic pressure — fast Piercing cd is the civ-archetype-as-driver expression in the per-tower magnitude direction. Without this, Greek Piercing would land at the same cd as Norse Piercing (Longship Dock — to be authored in R4), and the civ-archetype distinction would collapse. The same logic applies to Acropolis at long-range siege cadence (Greek's only Crushing source; the slow heavy variant) vs Norse Crushing (Mead Hall in R4 — likely faster cadence to match summon-anchor identity).

**Loop 3 — synthesis.** The variance-direction argument holds — `cd` and `range` bands ARE doing per-tower work as expression of civ-archetype-driven per-tier-archetype-role variance, not as raw "we picked numbers in band." The `controlled_by: leonidas` tag does per-civ work (correct), and the per-tower fields do per-tower work within the civ. The two layers cohere. **Validation criterion for RN:** if R3 Aztec and R4 Norse cd / range bands meaningfully differ from Greek's bands (Aztec dps-band-center higher at T2-T3 + cd similar / Norse cd-band-center lower + Bleed-stack status-proc magnitude higher), the civ-archetype-as-driver hypothesis is empirically validated at the per-tower magnitude layer. If R3/R4 bands look identical to Greek's, the hypothesis is hollow and RN audit will flag it. R2 commits a falsifiable per-civ magnitude signature for cross-arc parity.

**Synthesis-locked output:** Author all 15 rows with `controlled_by: leonidas` lane tag, dps-band variance ±10% off tier baseline expressing kinetic-vs-utility civ-archetype-role split, cd-band 0.8–2.6s expressing rapid-vs-siege per-tier-archetype-role split, range-band 3–5.5 cells centered moderate (Greek lockdown wants sustained engagement, not extreme-range or extreme-melee). Validation gates at RN cross-civ matrix.
