### 2. `identity_hook`

Greek is the **lockdown / control civilization** — its identity reads through the temporal compounding of moderate-cadence procs against high-HP targets held immobile inside Leonidas's Last-Stand zone. The per-tower magnitude signature centers (dps median ±10% off the §4.11.5 ladder; cd median 1.7s — slowest of three civs; range median 4.0 — middle of three civs) express *sustained pressure over peak burst*: Piercing armor-shred refreshes every 3s on a 1.2s-cd Phalanx, Crushing stagger refreshes every 1s on a 2.4s-cd Acropolis, Arcane hex refreshes every 2s on a 1.8s-cd Oracle, Fire burn DoT runs 3s on a 2.0s-cd Colossus, and Divine smite fires once per shot at 4-of-15 source density layered with the 4-cell Last-Stand 12s hard-stun. The status-proc archetype is **kinetic suppression** — five distinct deceleration vectors (slow / stun / armor-shred / stagger / hex-cast-slow) compound on a single moving enemy and approach an effective stop-state by the time it enters the Last-Stand zone, where Leonidas's once-per-match free signature converts the suppression stack into a literal 12s zero-mobility kill-floor. The matchup_affinity profile (STRONG vs Colossal + Shielded, WEAK vs Beast) is *the same shape* as the lockdown archetype's natural geometry: lockdown procs return their highest leverage on slow high-HP targets (Colossal R10/R15/R30 spikes; Shielded heavy-elite walls) and their lowest leverage on fast-Beast swarms — and Greek's per-tower magnitudes don't fight this geometry but lean into it via the Divine source-density (4 of 15 + 2 partials, heaviest in any civ) and zero-Poison liability. Leonidas's three abilities each target a different point in the suppression cycle: Spartan-Training's 15% civ-aura slow is the *passive baseline* (every Greek tower's procs land on already-decelerated targets); This Is Sparta!'s 4s hard-stun + 1-cell knockback is the *active spike* (resets enemy positioning into tower kill-zones); The Last Stand's 12s persistent zone-denial is the *signature window* (12s of guaranteed proc-saturation against any target inside the 4-cell radius). Read together — civ-aggregate matchup leaning toward Colossal/Shielded specialization, per-tower magnitudes centered on temporal-compounding procs, status-proc archetype dominated by deceleration vectors, commander synergy converting suppression-stack into immobility — Greek is mechanically identifiable as the civ where *the longer an enemy lives, the more procs it eats, and the slower it moves*, and where the player's optimal play is **routing high-HP threats through Greek-tower clusters and saving Last-Stand for the R30 Colossal spike**.

### 3. `signature_creep_types`

Greek specializes against **Colossal** and **Shielded** armor classes (archetype-grain only — per-creep-row specifics defer to the §4.7 enemy direction lock). The pairing reads cleanly: Colossal is the +25% Divine sink (Parthenon / Perseus / Jason / Zeus / Athena all benefit) and the smite +10%-true secondary, while Shielded is the +25% Piercing + Crushing convergence (Phalanx / Trireme Dock / Achilles + Acropolis / Hercules — the 7-of-15 paired-counter density). Both armor classes share the *high-HP / slow-target / single-priority-threat* shape that lockdown procs convert into compound advantage; both naturally surface at boss-tier waves (R10 / R15 / R30) and elite-armor waves (mid-round Shielded mini-bosses per the deferred §4.7 archetype roster). Greek-against-Colossal is the strongest civ × armor cell at launch (Divine source-density 6 of 15 effective + zero Poison liability + Divine smite proc layer). Greek-against-Shielded is the densest paired-counter cell (Piercing + Crushing = 7 favorable sources of 15 — the highest dual-counter density of any civ × armor cell at launch). Greek does *not* specialize against Spirit (the matchup has favorable Arcane + Divine answers but the Piercing source-density penalizes — net neutral); Unarmored (tier-asymmetric — late-tier Slashing answer + early-tier Crushing liability); or Beast (signature gap, see field 4).

### 4. `multi_civ_play_hook`

Greek's structural type-hole at the launch-roster grain is **Poison** (zero sources across 15 towers) and **Slashing pre-T4** (no T1-T3 mainline Slashing source — Theseus T4 is the earliest Greek Slashing). The Poison-hole resolves to a Beast-armor matchup gap (Beast is +25% Poison and +25% Fire, but Greek brings only 1 Fire source of 15 — so Greek's Beast-coverage is limited to ~7% of its tower roster) and a Colossal-secondary gap (Poison −25% vs Colossal is normally a liability, but Greek's zero-Poison means zero liability there — the hole hurts vs Beast, not vs Colossal). The Slashing-pre-T4 hole resolves to an Unarmored-tier-asymmetric matchup (T1-T3 Greek-vs-Unarmored relies on raw throughput, not RPS-favorable cells; T4+ normalizes). Cross-civ play fills both holes: **Aztec Poison-stacking** (Sacrificial Altar + Blood Priest + Tezcatlipoca, 3 of 15 sources at +25% Beast, sole stacking-proc exception per 2026-04-26 lock) directly answers the Beast gap; **Norse Slashing-density** (Longhouse + Berserker Lodge + Björn Ironside + Lagertha + Tyr, 5 of 15 Slashing sources — densest Slashing roster of any civ) directly answers the Unarmored-tier-asymmetric hole. The deferred Foresight-coin civ-pair model (Phase 4 §4.x, OPEN) and the Follow-up #6 patch-1 commander expansion (per-civ patch commanders bringing additional type-coverage) are the structural release valves for these holes — Greek-only single-civ play remains intentionally walled by Beast-heavy and Unarmored-T1-T3-heavy wave compositions, and the design intent (per the per-tower RN audit's "type-holes are not bugs" verdict and per the 2026-04-25 ratification's multi-civ-future commitment) is that *Greek's type-holes are the gameplay reason to engage cross-civ play, not a balance debt to patch*.

### 5. `commander_synergy`

**Leonidas (Control lane lock, Hard reversibility)** binds Greek's per-tower temporal-compounding lockdown stack (Piercing-shred 3s refresh / Crushing-stagger 1s / Arcane-hex 2s / Fire-burn 3s DoT / Divine-smite per-shot) into a three-stage suppression cycle — Spartan-Training's 15% civ-aura slow as passive baseline (all 15 Greek towers within 2-cell hex-distance of the anointed tower fire procs onto already-decelerated targets), This Is Sparta!'s 4s hard-stun + 1-cell knockback at 30s CD as the active spike (re-routes enemy positioning into Greek tower clusters), The Last Stand's 12s persistent 4-cell zone-denial as the once-per-match free signature window (converts the suppression stack into a literal zero-mobility kill-floor; canonical pairing is Last-Stand × Zeus 5-cell range overlap = ~13,200 raw damage in zone over 12s plus stagger × smite alternation against Colossal R30-boss for ~14,500–15,500 effective damage per the per-tower R2 spec) — and the cycle's three-stage shape reads as the same shape as Greek's matchup_affinity (slow-target-favorable matchups + lockdown-proc temporal-compounding + zero-Poison-liability against Colossal) at the per-commander interface layer.

## 3x debug loop on identity_hook construction

**Loop 1 — aggressive critique.** The identity_hook paragraph is a long synthesis sentence that *names* every input field (matchup_affinity Colossal/Shielded specialization; per-tower cd median 1.7s + dps centered + Divine source-density 4 of 15; status-proc archetype as "kinetic suppression"; Leonidas three-stage suppression cycle) but doesn't actually do mechanical work that the four other fields aren't already doing on their own. A reader who reads matchup_affinity + signature_creep_types + multi_civ_play_hook + commander_synergy gets the same picture without the identity_hook paragraph at all — the synthesis is *narrative glue*, not load-bearing schema content. Worse: the identity_hook claims "the longer an enemy lives, the more procs it eats, and the slower it moves" — but this is true of *any* civ with status-procs (Aztec Poison-stacking + Fire-burn DoT compounds across enemy lifespan; Norse Bleed-density propagates through Heathen-Army cleave with similar lifespan-compounding). Greek's "lockdown" framing isn't *uniquely Greek* at the proc-layer — it's the proc-density / proc-mix / proc-cadence that distinguishes Greek, and those are already in the per-tower R2 + RN audits. The identity_hook risks being prose-decoration that drifts into Follow-up #5 surface direction territory ("kinetic suppression," "five distinct deceleration vectors," "zero-mobility kill-floor" — these are mechanical-content phrasings, but the cumulative tone is starting to *evoke* Spartan-formation imagery without explicitly invoking it).

**Loop 2 — steelman.** The identity_hook is *defined* in R1 scope as a one-paragraph synthesis of the other four fields — its load-bearing job is precisely to be the integration artifact that reads the four fields as one mechanical thesis, not to introduce new content. The "narrative glue" critique would apply to any synthesis paragraph anywhere; it's a property of synthesis, not a defect. The "every civ has lifespan-compounding procs" critique is correct in isolation but misses the *shape* claim: Greek's lifespan-compounding is **deceleration-weighted** (slow + stun + stagger + hex-cast-slow + armor-shred-as-shred-refresh-not-DoT-stack), Aztec's is **kill-multiplication-weighted** (Poison-stacking + Fire-splash + Slashing-bleed compound on per-kill yield, not on per-target-lifespan), and Norse's is **summon-cleave-propagation-weighted** (Bleed-density propagating through Heathen-Army summons via cleave mechanics). The three civs' lifespan-compounding shapes are mechanically distinct, and Greek's deceleration-weighting is the lockdown archetype at the proc-layer. The "Spartan-formation imagery drift" critique is the genuine surface-direction risk — but the paragraph as written stays at mechanical-content language ("deceleration vectors," "compound," "saturation," "kill-floor") without invoking pose / VO / cultural surface direction; the ambient tone that *evokes* Spartan-formation is the locked content-skeleton names (Phalanx, Acropolis, Spartan-Training, Last Stand, Leonidas) doing their work, not new prose direction. Follow-up #5's hard-gate target is **new** civ-flavor surface direction prose, not the verbatim re-use of locked content-skeleton names in mechanical-content context.

**Loop 3 — synthesis.** The identity_hook does load-bearing work by naming the *shape* of Greek's lifespan-compounding (deceleration-weighted) and distinguishing it from Aztec (kill-multiplication-weighted) and Norse (summon-cleave-weighted) at a grain that the four field-rows individually don't reach — matchup_affinity is per-armor-class, signature_creep_types is per-armor-class subset, multi_civ_play_hook is per-type-hole, commander_synergy is per-ability-slot — none of which surfaces the *lifespan-compounding shape* that ties civ-archetype to per-commander-interface to matchup-geometry into one legible mechanical thesis. The narrative-glue critique is honest about synthesis being synthesis; the cross-civ proc-shape distinction (Greek deceleration / Aztec kill-multiplication / Norse summon-cleave) is the load-bearing payload that R3 + R4 will reciprocally surface at their own identity_hook layers. The Spartan-formation-drift risk is mitigated by holding mechanical-content language throughout — the locked content-skeleton names carry whatever cultural ambient is permissible per the 2026-04-25 ratification, and no new direction prose is introduced. **Validation criterion for RN cross-civ audit:** if R3 Aztec identity_hook surfaces a *kill-multiplication-weighted* lifespan-compounding shape and R4 Norse identity_hook surfaces a *summon-cleave-propagation-weighted* shape, the three civs' identity_hooks are empirically distinct at the proc-shape layer (not just at the matchup_affinity / commander layer). If R3/R4 identity_hooks land on the same deceleration-weighted shape as Greek, the schema's identity_hook field is hollow and RN audit flags it. R2 commits a falsifiable per-civ proc-shape distinction.

**Synthesis-locked output:** Hold the identity_hook paragraph as authored. The deceleration-weighted lifespan-compounding shape is Greek's load-bearing proc-shape claim, falsifiable at RN against Aztec's kill-multiplication-weighted and Norse's summon-cleave-propagation-weighted shapes. Mechanical-content language verified throughout — locked content-skeleton names reused verbatim, no new civ-flavor surface direction prose introduced.

## Reason chosen

Three reinforcing reasons hold the 5-field Greek profile together:

1. **The matchup_affinity / signature_creep_types / multi_civ_play_hook triplet is internally self-consistent** — Greek's STRONG cells (Colossal + Shielded) are the per-tower R2 + RN audit's empirical Divine-and-Piercing-density observations; Greek's WEAK cell (Beast) is the per-tower RN audit's empirical Poison-zero + Fire-light observation; the multi_civ_play_hook cross-references Aztec Poison and Norse Slashing as the predicted complementary fills, which R3 + R4 will reciprocally validate. No field contradicts another.
2. **The identity_hook synthesizes per-commander R5 + per-tower R2 + status-proc archetype into one mechanical thesis** with a falsifiable proc-shape claim (deceleration-weighted lifespan-compounding) that R3 Aztec and R4 Norse will reciprocally test (predicted kill-multiplication-weighted and summon-cleave-propagation-weighted). The identity_hook 3x debug loop confirms the synthesis isn't redundant with the other four fields — it does proc-shape distinction work that the field-row layer doesn't reach.
3. **The commander_synergy field cleanly resolves to Leonidas (Control lane lock, Hard reversibility)** with no cross-civ tag — every Greek tower's `controlled_by: leonidas` per-tower R2 row carries up to the civ-aggregate layer without polluting Aztec or Norse profiles (R3 / R4 will independently resolve to Montezuma II / Ragnar without crossing back to Leonidas). The lane-lock discipline scales from per-tower (45/45 verbatim per RN) to per-civ (15/15 per this round) without mismatch.
