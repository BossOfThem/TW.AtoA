## Decision

Ash to Altar's load-bearing differentiation in the tower-wars-and-adjacent design space rests on **three "where we win" axes** and **three "we are not trying to win on these" axes**. AtoA wins on (T1) **three-layer authored identity-density per civ** — civ-hook + tower-role + Fusion-product as simultaneous load-bearing identity layers, denser at the per-civ unit-of-analysis than any comparator's faction-equivalent across the 8-set; (T3) **mechanic novelty in the Fusion 9-recipe authored-lock paired with the 7-type attack/armor RPS lattice**, an authored-grammar contrast to ETD2's emergent-combinatorial element-pair space and SquadTD's emergent build-orders; and (T4) **two-mode-at-depth scope-fit on a shared Tower-defense substrate**, strictly more scope-ambitious than Path of Achra's solo-dev single-mode existence-proof while remaining inside the proven solo / tiny-team band and below the BTD6 / Mindustry ceiling that requires a decade of live-service or open-source community to reach. AtoA explicitly does **not** pursue (C1) **modding / scripting surface** — Mindustry's open-source + Workshop + Logic-scripting stack is the genre's modding ceiling and is structurally out of reach for a solo / tiny-team paid-EA shop; (C2) **mode-roster breadth** — BTD6's seven-plus mode-types and TDS's six-to-eight mode-types are bought via decade-long live-service or F2P-treadmill investment a paid-EA tiny-team launch cannot match (the They Are Billions ~18-month EA → 1.0 mode-add datum is the empirical anchor); and (C3) **discoverability-substrate dominance** — TDS's Roblox-front-page algorithmic surfacing and SquadTD's SC2-Arcade-tab inheritance are platform-substrate advantages a Steam paid-EA launch cannot import without importing their monetization shapes, which the no-P2W lock explicitly refuses. Authored cross-coherence (the audited 36/36 finding) is the substrate that lets T1 and T3 hold up under play but is not itself a headline differentiator — it is the reason the headline holds.

## Context

Phase D of [`PROJECT-ARC.md`](../concept/PROJECT-ARC.md) chartered the differentiation swarm to answer: "On what specific axes does AtoA do better than the closest comparators in the tower-wars-and-adjacent design space, and on what specific axes do those comparators do better than AtoA?" The swarm produced a 9-axis × 8-comparator research substrate (R1-A WC3-lineage-and-polish-anchor triad; R1-B Map-Maker-and-F2P-lobby pair; R1-C substrate-modding-and-solo-dev-and-RTS-hybrid triad; alignment-check; R2-A thesis dialectician; R2-B counter-thesis dialectician), now consolidated as [`research/2026-04-29-tw-comparator-matrix.md`](../research/2026-04-29-tw-comparator-matrix.md).

The thesis matters now because **Phase F work item F.2 (Steam-page draft) feeds directly from this decision** — the storefront copy that translates AtoA's locked design substrate into player-facing pitch language must rest on a load-bearing differentiation paragraph that is honest about both edges and concessions. The underlying truth the swarm surfaced is a single sentence: **breadth is unreachable; depth is preserved.** This thesis articulates that truth into Phase-F-consumable form. The "breadth unreachable" half is empirically anchored (TaB's ~18-month single-mode-add datum from a small team with shipping experience); the "depth preserved" half is empirically anchored (Path of Achra at 98% / ~3k reviews / $9.99 / single dev as the existence-proof for solo-scale depth-over-breadth). The locked design substrate (3-civ × 6-tower × Fusion stack, 7-type attack/armor RPS, paid-base + day-1-BP cosmetic-only monetization, Tower + PvP-Co-op feature-complete-at-1.0 cut) sits inside that proven shape.

## Alternatives considered

- **Option A — Thesis without concession axes.** Lead with T1 + T3 + T4 only; omit C1 / C2 / C3 from the storefront-facing thesis. **Rejected:** loses honesty. The Steam page would over-claim against Mindustry's modding ceiling, BTD6's mode breadth, and TDS's discoverability, inviting comparator backlash from buyers who arrive expecting features AtoA does not ship. Per [`alignment-check.md`](../concept/atoa-vs-mtm/diff-swarm/alignment-check.md) §5 brief-to-R3, the "we win on these / we are not trying to win on these" two-column framing is the load-bearing posture.
- **Option B — Thesis with all 4 R2-A axes including T2 cross-coherence.** Lead with T1 + T2 + T3 + T4. **Rejected:** loses load-bearing focus. Per [`r2-a-thesis.md`](../concept/atoa-vs-mtm/diff-swarm/r2-a-thesis.md) §2 own loop-2 weakest-claim flag, T2 is internal-discipline-flavoured — un-felt by the player at first contact, only felt over many hours when contradictions don't surface. R2-A's own §3 loop-3 synthesis demoted T2 from headline to substrate-evidence under T1 and T3. Promoting it to headline weakens the thesis.
- **Option C — RT-3-triggered redesign on a counter-axis.** Treat the C1 / C2 / C3 findings as upstream-redesign signals — for example, scope-up a modding surface to contest Mindustry, or scope-up mode-count to contest BTD6 / TDS. **Rejected per [`r2-b-counter-thesis.md`](../concept/atoa-vs-mtm/diff-swarm/r2-b-counter-thesis.md) all-concession disposition.** Per CHARTER §6, RT-3 fires only on load-bearing dominance **AND** audience-priority confirmation; the audience for the post-cut Tower + PvP-Co-op shape is the LTD2 / Achra / ETD2 review-band reachable cluster (alignment-check §4 finding #4), for whom none of C1 / C2 / C3 is the buy-signal. C1 is single-comparator dominance without audience-priority confirmation; C2's breadth-cost is empirically affirmed not contradicted; C3 is platform-substrate not design-substrate. None trip RT-3.
- **Option D — Thesis as adopted (T1 + T3 + T4 wins, C1 + C2 + C3 concessions, T2 substrate-evidence).** **Chosen.** Three load-bearing wins, three honest concessions, one substrate-evidence layer.

## Reason chosen

**Loop 1 (aggressive critique).** The strongest attack on the chosen thesis is that T4 (two-mode-at-depth scope-fit) is fragile against the They Are Billions 18-month single-mode-add datum: if a small team with shipping experience took 18 months for one mode, can a solo / tiny team really ship two-modes-at-depth at 1.0? A second attack: T1's "three identity layers" claim collapses to two if a player reads Fusion as "tier 3 of tower-role" rather than as a third independent layer (per R2-A §2 secondary weakness flag) — and if T1 collapses to two layers, the comparator gap narrows toward BTD6's Paragon and ETD2's tri-element T3. A third attack: C3 (discoverability) is a marketing-substrate axis not a design-substrate one and may mis-categorize against the PREP §3 four-axis decomposition (per R2-B §2 weakest-claim flag), making it the soft target if any single concession axis is to be dropped.

**Loop 2 (steelman).** Loop 1 over-weights the TaB datum: TaB's two modes (Survival ↔ Campaign) required new RPG-Empire-metalayer authoring (per `r1-c §2 axis 4`), making them substrate-distinct rather than substrate-shared. AtoA's Tower + PvP-Co-op pair shares a single Tower-defense substrate × multiplayer skin — confirmed by Mindustry's pattern of four mode-types on one substrate (`r1-c §2 axis 9`). On T1, the "three layers vs two" question is empirically resolvable post-launch but the *authored intent* of three independent layers (civ-hook drives faction identity, tower-role drives in-match function, Fusion-product drives late-game composition) is what the design-substrate-locked-surface state already commits to per [`CONCEPT.md`](../CONCEPT.md) §§4 / 5.4 — and per R2-A §3 loop-3 defense, the comparator-relevant metric is layered identity per civ, not raw node count. On C3, R2-B §3 already chose option (a) — name the platform-substrate concession honestly — and per alignment-check §4 finding #2 the no-P2W posture is the natural counter-frame; demoting C3 to a footnote loses the load-bearing pairing with the cosmetic-only / paid-EA cluster norm.

**Loop 3 (synthesis).** The chosen three-and-three shape is the load-bearing one. T1 + T3 + T4 are the player-felt headline and substrate-novelty hook and scope-honesty frame respectively (per R2-A §4 brief-to-R3 ranking). C1 + C2 + C3 are the three honest concessions where the comparator field beats AtoA, framed not as fix-targets but as deliberate non-pursuits (per R2-B §3 loop-3 synthesis): C1 concedes to one comparator only (Mindustry single-comparator-anomaly per alignment-check §4 finding #1); C2 is empirically anchored on the TaB 18-month datum and counter-postured by the Achra existence-proof; C3 is platform-substrate not design-substrate and points to the no-P2W cluster posture rather than a defensive fix. T2 (cross-coherence 36/36) is substrate-evidence under T1 and T3 — it is the reason the headline holds up over many hours, not the headline itself. The thesis is cross-coherent with the alignment-check §5 brief on both sides: anchored on `r1-a §2 axis 2` (BTD6 + ETD2 identity-density), `r1-b §2 axis 2` (SquadTD per-builder racial passive), `r1-a §2 axis 3` (ETD2 element-pair grammar), `r1-b §1` (SquadTD emergent build-orders), `r1-c §1, §2 axis 9` (Achra existence-proof + Mindustry shared-substrate pattern), `r1-c §2 axis 8` (Mindustry modding ceiling), `r1-c §2 axis 9` (TaB 18-month mode-add cost), `r1-b §1, §2 axis 5` (TDS Roblox front-page + SquadTD SC2-Arcade discoverability).

## Reversibility note

**Easy.** This thesis is articulation, not architectural lock — it describes what AtoA *is* given the locked design substrate, it does not modify any locked surface. Downstream Phase F Steam-page drafts iterate on framing (which axes to lead, how to phrase concessions, how to integrate with cultural-sensitivity gate language) without touching locked surfaces. If a future comparator survey or audience-priority signal reveals a different load-bearing differentiation, the thesis paragraph is rewriteable; the Phase F Steam-page copy is rewriteable. No upstream cascade impact. The cost of undoing this decision is the cost of rewriting one paragraph in the Steam-page draft and one decision file — measured in hours, not days.
