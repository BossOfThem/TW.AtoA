## Decision (candidate world statement)

A single-paragraph world pitch for the synthetic mythic-history braid direction. One candidate — not an option matrix — because Q2 is meant to produce something PM can iterate on, not a menu to pick from. If this misses, the 3x debug loop below shows where the load-bearing moves are so PM can redirect the specific move that's wrong rather than discard the whole draft.

### One-paragraph world statement (v0.1)

> In the world — working-name **[PLACEHOLDER]** — reality is sedimented belief. Mountains hold because they were agreed on longest; daylight falls at its angle because an ancient consortium of meaning-makers ruled it so, and nothing since has mustered the coherence to overrule them. Deep physics is old, settled, uncontested. But the upper layers of the world — who rules, which rites bind, which heroes existed, which laws of war apply on this particular field — are perpetually in argument, and a well-built structure, faithfully used, can shift them. This world is described in four vocabularies that are not four worlds but one substance: **history** is what people built and chronicled, **religion** is what people ritualized and consecrated, **mythology** is what people told and populated with heroes and horrors, and **philosophy** is what people argued into coherence. The braid is that no vocabulary stands free: a temple without a doctrine collapses; a doctrine without a rite evaporates; a rite without a myth finds no one to perform it; a myth without a monument fades. A **Commander** is a rare figure credentialed to edit the world across all four vocabularies at once — to erect a structure that is simultaneously an argument, a rite, a told story, and a historical claim. The Commander stands on a field where something is being contested — by enemies whose very existence is a rival ontological assertion — and by the end of the engagement the world is, in some small and durable way, different: more one thing, less another. **Towers** are doctrines rendered architecturally; **spells** are rites performed correctly enough to bind; **enemies** are incursions of what-might-have-been-believed-instead.

### Core moves the statement makes (flagged so PM can contest individually)

1. **Substrate-claim.** Reality is sedimented belief. Physics is the oldest, un-contestable sediment; only the upper layers are in play.
2. **Four vocabularies, one substance.** History / religion / mythology / philosophy are not four genres pasted together — they're four ways of describing the same ontological material. This is the move that earns the word *braid* and differentiates this pitch from remix or pastiche.
3. **Mutual-dependence rule.** Temple needs doctrine; doctrine needs rite; rite needs myth; myth needs monument. The four strands *hold each other up.* Any lineage or tower that only expresses one vocabulary is structurally unstable.
4. **Commander-as-editor.** The Commander's distinguishing trait is credentialing across *all four* vocabularies simultaneously. Not a priest, not a philosopher, not a king, not a bard — someone who can sign off on all four at once for a brief authorized window (a match). This gives the Commander a diegetic reason to exist, be rare, be powerful, and be the player.
5. **Tower-as-doctrine-rendered.** Towers aren't weapons adjacent to the world-frame — they *are* the world-frame's load-bearing units. The TD mechanic becomes the world's native shape, not a layer on top.
6. **Enemy-as-rival-ontology.** Enemies are not generic monsters. They exist because they are being believed in — somewhere, by something — and the Commander's job is to argue them out of existence by the field going silent. Leaves debuff and magic mechanics with clean native meaning (doctrinal challenge; compelled rite).
7. **Match-as-irreversible-edit.** Each engagement measurably, durably alters which beliefs held on that field. Implies persistence hooks (seasonal? campaign? tier? — deferred to Q5 age-model per reopen).

### What this pitch DOES NOT say (deliberate gaps)

- **Lineage count.** Not implied. The four vocabularies are the *descriptive substrates*, not a lineage count. Lineages could be 2, 3, 4, 5, N — Q3 decides.
- **Hybrid topology.** Not decided. The mutual-dependence rule (move 3) suggests hybrid topology could be very rich, but Q6 decides whether hybrids are enabler-based, any-pair, or something else native to the frame.
- **Commander count / roster shape.** Not implied. Q3 decides.
- **Named pantheon, specific geography, specific eras.** Deliberately empty. A world pitch is the *rule for generating* these, not a first instance of them.
- **Match arc / campaign arc.** Q4 decides.
- **Economy.** Q7 decides — though the frame hints ("coherence" as scarce, "credential" as spendable) without committing.
- **Title.** Q8 decides — "Ash to Altar" is no longer load-bearing.
- **Tone/visual aesthetic.** Flagged as a follow-up; the pitch is ontological-structural, not sensory. A sensory pass is its own step.

## Alternatives considered (framings Q2 could have taken — not chosen for this draft)

- **Option A — Pantheon-first.** Start with a named pantheon and let history/philosophy descend from it. Rejected: collapses braid → religion-dominant; other three strands become flavor, not load-bearing. Violates "braid, not side-by-side."
- **Option B — Era-ladder-first.** Start with a named historical era spine (bronze → iron → classical → etc.) under myth veneer. Rejected: is exactly the remix direction the reopen explicitly rejected.
- **Option C — Place-first.** Start with a specific geography (e.g., a city on a coast between two empires) and let the four strands grow out of its conflicts. Rejected *for this draft* but noted as a strong follow-up — a place-first pass could be Q2.5 if the ontological frame here lands but feels un-grounded. (See Follow-up #3.)
- **Option D — Commander-first.** Start with the Commander's diegetic role and build the world around what they do. Rejected: premature — that's closer to Q3 (Actors). Doing it at Q2 would force lineage/roster commitments too early.
- **Option E — Ontological frame (chosen).** Start with what *kind of thing* the world is (sedimented belief, four vocabularies of one substance, mutual dependence). Lets Q3/Q6 land cleanly; gives enemies and towers native meaning; differentiates from cliché "belief makes gods" by inverting it (belief makes ground; gods are byproducts).

## Reason chosen + 3x debug loop

Ontological-frame first, because the reopen's rejected alternatives (remix, pastiche, multiverse) are all *presentation* frames. The only way to avoid falling back into them under pressure is to commit to a *structural* rule for how the world's substance works, then let presentation flow from that rule.

### Loop 1 — aggressive critique

1. **"Belief makes reality" is one of the most-used tropes in fantasy.** Discworld (small gods), Gaiman (American Gods), Unsong, Xenogears, Sandman. Is this "synthetic" or just "trope with serial numbers filed off"?
2. **If every tower is a doctrine and every spell is a rite, gameplay risks feeling samey.** How does a Sinew-analog tower feel different from a Prayer-analog tower if they're both "an argument rendered architecturally"?
3. **"History" collapses into "religion" and "mythology" collapses into "religion" in practice.** Four vocabularies look clean on paper; in a shipped game, priests-philosophers-monks-bards blur. The braid becomes a two-ply.
4. **Where is material reality?** Geography, economy, climate, technology, survival pressure — the pitch says "physics is old sediment" but gives no handle on how a player encounters the *concrete* world. A world pitch that has no place to stand isn't a world; it's a metaphysics essay.
5. **This is extremely abstract.** A player on the title screen needs tone, sensory anchor, place. "Reality is sedimented belief" is not a place.
6. **Commander-as-editor-of-the-world is power-fantasy maximalism.** If the Commander can edit four vocabularies of reality, why do they need towers at all? Why don't they just rewrite the enemy out of existence?
7. **Enemies as "rival ontological assertions" is abstract and hard to design silhouettes around.** What does a tower kill when it kills an enemy? If the answer is "a competing belief," that's hard to read at gameplay speed.
8. **The "mutual-dependence rule" (temple/doctrine/rite/myth/monument) is seductive prose but mechanically unclear.** Does it mean a tower needs adjacency to all four strand-types to function? That's a huge constraint on TD layout.
9. **Pretentious-risk is real.** "Perpetual argument about what reality should be" reads as grad-school thesis. A TD player wants to place towers, not read Foucault.
10. **"Philosophers argue / priests ritualize / kingdoms build / mythographers tell" implies four factions.** That pre-commits a lineage count (four) the reopen explicitly left open.

### Loop 2 — steelman

- **(1) cliché charge:** The cliché is *belief makes gods.* This pitch inverts it: *belief makes the ground; gods are a byproduct of sustained coordination, not the primary object.* Unsong is closest but kabbalistic, not civilizational. Discworld's small gods are comedic, never load-bearing. If we commit to "the laws of nature are the oldest sediment of coordinated belief," that's genuinely not a trope I can recall being load-bearing in a TD. The *inversion* is the synthetic move.
- **(2) samey-gameplay charge:** Solved by giving each vocabulary a native *grammar* for how its doctrines express. Argument-grammar = formal proof, projectiles as syllogisms, long-range precision. Rite-grammar = sanctification, aura, slow consecrating damage over zone. Story-grammar = naming-the-enemy, lockout / debuff / crowd-control. Monument-grammar = endurance, tank, blocker. These are wildly different feels, not variations on one feel. The vocabulary *is* the mechanical identity.
- **(3) history/myth collapse charge:** Real — if we leave the four vocabularies undifferentiated. Fix: commit explicit roles. **History = what persists in record** (towers as monument, memory, archive). **Religion = what is performed** (rite, sacrament, consecration). **Mythology = what is populated** (heroes, monsters, named entities). **Philosophy = what is argued** (law, theorem, principle). History is the *chronicle* layer (did it happen?). Religion is the *performance* layer (is it still being done?). Mythology is the *personnel* layer (who's in the story?). Philosophy is the *argument* layer (is it coherent?). Four distinct operational axes, not a four-synonym stack.
- **(4) no material reality charge:** Steelmanned fix — material reality is the *deepest* layer of sedimented belief, so deep no contemporary faction can touch it. Geography, climate, day/night, gravity — all settled. What's contested in the upper layers is what *this place on this map* means, who lives here, what laws apply, what can happen. This preserves a grounded world (you can stand on a hill, feel wind, cross a river) while keeping the upper layers in play. The material world is *sediment showing*; the Commander fights on its surface.
- **(5) abstract-no-sensory charge:** Legitimate gap. A sensory pass is a follow-up. The world pitch here is the *generating rule*; a specific place-pass ("the Commander stands on [a terraced hillside of weathered stone, air sour with incense, wind off a coast where something old washes up every decade]") is Q2.5 territory. Noting as Follow-up #3.
- **(6) why-towers-if-editor charge:** Solved by the credentialing constraint. The Commander is *licensed* to edit — but the edit is laborious, expensive, and requires the four vocabularies' native instruments to actually manifest. A Commander *without* towers/spells/rites is just a person with credentials and no tools. The Commander is a conductor, not a solo act. The field is where the edit actually gets *made*, not where it's decreed.
- **(7) enemy-silhouette charge:** Partially real. Fix: enemies aren't pure abstractions — they're *what the rival ontology populates the world with.* A rival mythology sends heroes-gone-wrong, aberrant beasts, half-real gods. A rival philosophy sends constructs that obey only the rival's logic (can't be harmed by non-conforming damage types). A rival religion sends the consecrated of another rite. Lots of silhouette space. The *reason* an enemy exists is ontological; *what* it looks like is mythographic.
- **(8) mutual-dependence mechanical charge:** Real. The four-strand mutual-dependence rule is *flavor, not gameplay constraint*, unless Q6 (hybrid topology) decides otherwise. Draft should not commit it as a TD layout constraint. The rule explains *why the world coheres*, not *why a tower works in isolation on a map.* Noted as Follow-up #4.
- **(9) pretentious-risk charge:** Real and should be addressed at the presentation layer (tone pass, UX copy pass). The world-pitch can be abstract because it's a *design document*, not an in-game narrator. Follow-up #5.
- **(10) four-faction implication charge:** Reworded in the draft to frame the four vocabularies as *descriptive substrates*, not factional slots. The "What this pitch DOES NOT say" section makes this explicit: lineage count is Q3, not prefigured.

### Loop 3 — synthesis

The pitch is **structurally sound** if:

- **(a)** The four vocabularies are kept *operationally distinct* (chronicle / performance / personnel / argument) rather than synonym-stacked.
- **(b)** The inversion (belief → ground, not belief → gods) is held firmly — because that inversion is the synthetic move that earns the word *synthetic* in the reopen's thematic direction.
- **(c)** The frame is understood as *generative* — i.e., it tells us how to answer Q3–Q10 consistently, not what the answers are.
- **(d)** A sensory/place pass (Q2.5) follows Q2, before Q3, so the world has a place to stand before we start populating it.
- **(e)** The mutual-dependence rule is held as *world-coherence flavor* at Q2 stage, with its mechanical load-bearing (if any) deferred to Q6.

Actionable direction: file this as Proposed. PM reacts by (i) redirecting specific moves #1–#7 that miss, (ii) accepting the frame and moving to Q3, (iii) requesting Q2.5 sensory-pass before Q3, or (iv) rejecting frame entirely and asking for a different Q2 draft (e.g., Option C place-first or Option D commander-first).

Stop-gaps: if moves #2 (four-vocabularies-one-substance), #4 (Commander-as-cross-vocabulary-editor), or #6 (tower-as-doctrine-rendered) are rejected, the pitch collapses and Q2 restarts. If only surface moves are rejected (#1 specific phrasings, #5 tower/spell/enemy re-labelings, #7 match persistence hook), the pitch survives a revision.
