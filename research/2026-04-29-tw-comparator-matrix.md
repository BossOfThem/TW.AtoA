### Per-axis 1-line summary rows

- **Axis 1 — Units.** Mobile-roster shape splits sharply: tower-morph (LTD2, SquadTD), tower-only with creep-as-enemy (BTD6, ETD2, TDS), factory-produced (Mindustry, TaB), or no roster (Achra).
- **Axis 2 — Structures.** Identity-density anchors at three loci: per-tower (BTD6, TDS), per-faction-pair (ETD2 elements, SquadTD builders), or per-block-combinatorial (Mindustry).
- **Axis 3 — Tiers.** Cost-grammar splits into stat-stair (BTD6 Paragon), compositional (ETD2 element-combo), throughput-unlock (Mindustry), or horizontal-build (Achra).
- **Axis 4 — Progression.** Three meta-progression archetypes: heavy-tree (BTD6 Knowledge), gacha-treadmill (TDS), or thin-cosmetic (LTD2/ETD2/Mindustry/Achra/TaB).
- **Axis 5 — Lobby/MP.** Discoverability stratifies cleanly: platform-tab (SquadTD/SC2), algorithmic-front-page (TDS/Roblox), first-party matchmaking (LTD2/BTD6/ETD2), or zero-MP (Achra/TaB).
- **Axis 6 — Monetization.** Cosmetic-only / paid-DLC is the WC3-lineage and indie norm; soft-P2W (BTD6 Powers, TDS Golden Perks) is the live-service / F2P norm.
- **Axis 7 — Replay/observer.** Thinnest evidence axis bucket-wide; only SquadTD (SC2-native), LTD2 (community-leveraged), and ETD2 (marketed feature) ship a meaningful surface.
- **Axis 8 — Modding.** **Single-comparator anomaly:** only Mindustry ships a first-party Workshop + scripting + open-source stack; SquadTD's editor-power is the host platform's, not the product's.
- **Axis 9 — Mode-roster B×D.** Breadth-and-depth ceiling (BTD6 / TDS) bought via decade of live-service or F2P-treadmill; depth-over-breadth (Achra, LTD2) is the proven solo / small-team success path.

## §2 — Key data points (consolidated callouts, all 8 comparators)

- **Legion TD 2.** Standalone sequel to 2009 WC3 mod; Steam ~$9.99 + paid Campaign DLCs (Floating Isles, Desert Ridge); ~14k+ Steam reviews at ~87% positive ("Very Positive"); 100+ fighters across 10+ Legions; 1v1/2v2/4v4 + co-op vs AI; cosmetic-only IAP (explicit no-P2W); no first-party Workshop.
- **Bloons TD 6.** NinjaKiwi live-service sequel (2018, still updating 2026); Steam $13.99; ~225k+ reviews at ~97% positive ("Overwhelmingly Positive") — genre review-weight ceiling; 23 Monkey Towers + 16+ Heroes; 70+ maps; Co-Op 4p + Boss / Race / Odyssey / Contested Territory live events; Trophy Store cosmetics + consumable Powers (soft-P2W critique); no mod tools.
- **Element TD 2.** Element Studios 2021 standalone successor (5M+ historical mod downloads); Steam $14.99 + Mazing Expansion DLC; ~1.6k Steam reviews at ~90% positive; 6 elements → 59 towers; 28-mission singleplayer campaign + 8p co-op + ranked 1v1; 26 maps × 55-wave gauntlet w/ endless boss; 10 modifiers + replays + leaderboards + cross-region servers; no Workshop.
- **SC2 Squadron TD.** Free SC2 Arcade map (~2011, WC3-Legion-TD lineage 2009); 1-8p two-team Security-System defense over 31+ waves; ~9-12 named builders with racial passives (Ghost = Ethereal Cloaking 7.5% evade); build-phase static towers morph to AI fighters in combat; Arcade Mode unlock-bypass; no price, no IAP, no Steam-comparable review surface; full Galaxy Editor power inherited.
- **Tower Defense Simulator (Roblox).** Paradoxum Games (June 2019); 4.65B+ visits / 3.3M favorites cumulative early 2026; F2P + dense IAP (VIP gamepass, VIP+ $4.99/mo, Premium/Deluxe/Golden Skincrates with grind-or-Robux Golden Perks stat-buffs, BP 499 Robux, paid level-skip 32+ Robux/level); ~30+ towers across rarity tiers (Saboteur added April 2026); 6-8 modes (Standard/Molten/Fallen/Hardcore-L50/PWII-L50 + rotating events).
- **Mindustry.** Solo-developer Anuken (Anuke); $9.99 Steam + free on itch.io; ~25k+ Steam reviews at ~96% positive ("Overwhelmingly Positive"); 1M-2M owners per SteamSpy; open-source codebase; Steam Workshop + Java/JSON content modding + Mindustry Logic in-game programmable processors; campaign sectors + custom + PvP teams + co-op survival on shared substrate.
- **Path of Achra.** Solo developer Ulfsire; $9.99 Steam (regularly $4.99 sale); ~3k Steam reviews at ~98% positive; 1000+ culture × class × religion combinations; 50+ elementally-aligned powers; broken-build sandbox identity; **single mode** authored to extreme combinatorial depth — the existence-proof for solo-dev depth-over-breadth.
- **They Are Billions.** Numantian Games (small team, not solo); $29.99 Steam; ~18.7k reviews at ~80% positive ("Mostly Positive"); Survival shipped December 2017 EA; "New Empire" 40-50h hand-crafted campaign shipped at 1.0 June 18 2019 — **~18 months EA → 1.0**, with publicly extended timeline mid-build when campaign scope expanded. The canonical post-EA mode-add cost data point.

## §3 — Cross-bucket observations (5 named, ported from `alignment-check.md` §4)

1. **Modding-surface dominance is a single-comparator anomaly, not a genre norm.** Across all 8 comparators, only Mindustry ships first-party modding + Workshop + scripting (axis 8); SquadTD's editor-power is the host platform's (Galaxy) not the product's; the WC3-lineage triad (LTD2/BTD6/ETD2) ships zero modding surface despite tracing modding lineage. Conceding modding to Mindustry concedes to **one** comparator, not the field.

2. **Cosmetic-only / no-P2W is the WC3-lineage and indie norm; live-service P2W is the F2P-treadmill norm.** LTD2, ETD2, Mindustry, Achra, TaB all hold strict cosmetic-only-or-paid-DLC lines. BTD6 (consumable Powers) and TDS (Golden Perks stat-buff path) are the soft-P2W vectors — both are live-service / F2P shapes. A no-P2W lock is **norm-conformant** within the paid-EA cluster, not a differentiator within it.

3. **Post-EA mode-add cost is empirically expensive (TaB ~18 months for a small team).** The single most cite-worthy datum across all three buckets for the supersession's "breadth is unreachable" framing.

4. **Steam-review weight is brutally lopsided across the comparator set.** BTD6 ~225k @ 97% and Mindustry ~25k @ 96% sit one-to-two orders of magnitude above LTD2 (~14k @ 87%), TaB (~18.7k @ 80%), Achra (~3k @ 98%), ETD2 (~1.6k @ 90%). The reachable review-anchor band for a paid-EA solo / tiny-team launch is the LTD2 / Achra / ETD2 1k-15k band, not the BTD6 / Mindustry ceiling.

5. **Depth-over-breadth is a proven solo / small-team success path.** Path of Achra (1 mode, 98% positive, ~3k reviews, $9.99, single dev) is the cleanest existence-proof; LTD2 (narrow-roster competitive-PvP, 87% positive) is a second proof at the multiplayer-substrate end. The "1-2 mode-types at depth" posture has shipped successfully across both single-player and multiplayer ends of the comparator set.
