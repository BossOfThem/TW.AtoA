## Purpose

Does the DRG-style model (premium price + free earnable BP + cosmetic DLC) hold up for a two-dev strategy game in 2024–2026? What's a realistic revenue and ops envelope?

## To be written (Phase B pass — deep-dive)

### Sections to populate

1. **Benchmark titles — sustained performers.** Deep Rock Galactic, Vampire Survivors, Balatro, Against the Storm, Dome Keeper, Mechabellum, Legion TD 2, Hades II early access. Per title: launch price, ongoing revenue shape, content cadence, team size, live-ops burden.
2. **Small-team live-ops reality.** What have 2–5 person teams actually sustained for season cadence? Where have they burned out?
3. **Pay-to-win slip-case studies.** Titles where "cosmetic-only" slipped and community torched them. Reinforces the [CLAUDE.md](../CLAUDE.md) identity constraint with evidence.
4. **Season length benchmarks.** [PLAYER-JOURNEY §Battle Pass](../PLAYER-JOURNEY.md) places 10–12 weeks. Validate or challenge.
5. **Premium price anchor.** $5–$15 range for strategy-adjacent indie; is $10 or $15 the right anchor?
6. **Store refresh rates.** How often do comparable titles rotate cosmetics without burning the player base.
