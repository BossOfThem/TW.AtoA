# Agent 09 — Creator Complaint Atlas (Wave 1)

**Date:** 2026-04-27
**Scope:** Creator-side (streamer / YouTuber / podcaster) public complaints about TW / Tower-Wars / Hero / Horde / Lane mode-types and the games that ship them. Player-side complaints are Agent 8's beat — this report deliberately mines the *creator voice* register (rationalized-for-an-audience grievance).

## What creators complain about across the genre

Across Twitch VODs, YouTube essay-rants, Steam-discussion screencaps, and strategy-game podcast transcripts, creators covering TW-genre titles voice a tight cluster of grievances: the genre ships the same shape-of-game over and over with no portable customization layer (the SC1/WC3 lobby never came back), publishers actively *hostile* to creator/modder labor (Reforged TOS, hostbot removal, paywalled cosmetics in EA), live-service balance churn that makes guide-style content stale within 48 hours of upload, weak first-party tooling for the things creators actually need (replay sharing, observer mode, snapshot/clip export, lobby-driven viewer participation), and a Twitch/YouTube algorithm reality where TW-genre coverage underperforms FPS/MOBA/Survival in CCU and watch-time, forcing creators to either become single-game lifers or quit the niche. The articulate version of the complaint is almost always: *"the developer treats the creator as a marketing surface, not as a co-author of the game's longevity."*

## Creator-Complaint Taxonomy

1. **Custom-lobby grief.** SC1/WC3 lobby + map-share pipeline never reappeared in any modern TW. Reforged actively destroyed what was left (TOS IP-grab, hostbot removal). Cited as the #1 systemic loss.
2. **Content-creation friction.** Missing or weak replay export, no "share this lobby state" snapshot, no observer cam, no built-in clip/highlight tools. Creators end up screen-recording instead of using game-native pipes.
3. **Stagnation grievances.** "It's the same game shipped ten times." Especially loud around Bloons-style TD and Roblox-derivative TDs; creators say variations are cosmetic, not structural.
4. **Audience-economics complaints.** TW/TD content underperforms on Twitch CCU and YouTube CTR vs. mainstream genres; creators rationalize quitting or pivoting to "variety" with the algo as cover.
5. **Dev-relations breakdown.** Stormgate-era creators (Crank, etc.) called out dev defensiveness; Reforged-era creators called out radio silence. Pattern: devs read creator feedback as PR risk, not signal.
6. **Live-service / patch-treadmill fatigue.** Guide videos go stale; tier lists invalidated mid-week; creators describe being "balance-patched out of a livelihood."
7. **Customization-gap creator wishlist.** No streamer-mode UI (auto-hide MMR, mask matchmaking codes, chat-overlay friendly), no Twitch-extension hooks, no chat-plays-the-game rails baked in.
8. **Monetization-hostile patterns.** Stormgate "everything paywalled in EA"; Reforged "we own your map." Creators frame this as the publisher betraying the creator/player covenant.
9. **No portable replay/share format.** Even where replays exist they are version-locked, single-binary, no Twitch-clip equivalent — kills "look at this insane comeback" content.
10. **Genre-prestige decay.** Creators feel the TW/TD label itself is a viewership debuff; some explicitly rebrand their content to dodge the tag.

## Open questions for the PM

1. **Replay-as-shareable-asset:** Should AtoA ship a portable replay format (URL-shareable, version-tolerant, with a thumbnail/GIF auto-export) that no standalone TW currently has? What's the engineering cost vs. the creator-acquisition value?
2. **Custom-lobby IP stance:** Will AtoA publicly commit (in EULA + marketing) to *creators retain IP in custom games / mode-mods*? This is a near-zero-engineering differentiator with high creator-trust payoff post-Reforged.
3. **Streamer-mode + chat-plays hooks:** Should the lobby grammar include first-class Twitch-extension / chat-input slots — i.e. a lobby setting "chat votes on Fusion God draft" or "viewers spawn waves" — as part of the customization surface, not a bolt-on?
4. **Patch cadence philosophy:** Where on the spectrum from "weekly live-service patches" to "seasonal honest cadence" does AtoA land, and how do we communicate that posture to creators *before* launch so guide-content makers can plan?
5. **"What creator-friendly features could AtoA bake in that no standalone TW has?"** Concretely: lobby-snapshot share-codes, observer cam with director controls, in-engine GIF/clip export, MMR-masking streamer-mode toggle, Discord-RPC lobby invites, Twitch-extension overlay API, replay upload to a first-party browse-and-rate hub. Which of these are in scope for Phase-5 engine lock?
6. **Creator-relations posture:** Is there an explicit non-defensiveness commitment for public dev comms — i.e. an Ash-to-Altar version of "we will never blame the audience for our flop" — and does that need to be a written principle in CONCEPT.md?
