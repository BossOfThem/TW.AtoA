# Agent 04 — YouTube Longform & Content-Creator Lens

**Wave 1 / Agent 4 of 10 — Ash to Altar product-shape research**
**Date: 2026-04-27**
**Scope: Design-observation YouTube longform; novelty/customization-gap critiques; "what would make this genre actually click" essays. (Creator-complaint mining handled by Agent 9.)**

---

## Summary (1 paragraph)

The YouTube creator-critique landscape on Tower-genre design (TD, Tower Wars, Line TD, Hero/Horde-lineage maps) converges on a small, stable set of observations: (a) the modern shipped TD genre is treated as "solved" — once an optimal placement crystallizes, replay collapses, and creators reach for roguelite/RTS-hybrid grafts to reintroduce decision pressure; (b) the WC3/SC1 custom-lobby ecosystem is repeatedly framed as a never-replicated golden age — DotA, Wintermaul, Element TD, Legion TD, Footmen Frenzy, Vampirism, Island Defense are name-checked as proof that **mode-breadth + community moddability** generated something no standalone TW has captured; (c) the standout commercial successes (Bloons TD 6) win on *content surface area* — towers, maps, modes, co-op, races, bosses, odysseys — not mechanical novelty, suggesting "feature-complete coverage of every mode-type" is itself the underexplored design move; (d) hybrid-grafts (They Are Billions, Mindustry, Dawn of Defense) are where creators currently aim "this is what the genre needed" framing, but each hybridizes only one axis (RTS, factory, roguelite), leaving the multi-mode Ash-to-Altar bet uncontested. The recurring observation that maps to PROMISE-2 most loudly: nobody has shipped the lobby/customization layer that made WC3 the cultural reference point.

## Open questions for the PM

1. **Creator-channel target list.** For the demo / closed-beta phase, which creator archetype do we want to seed first — the Mark Brown / GMTK design-essay tier (slow, prestigious, cross-genre reach), the Mandalore / SsethTzeentach obscure-deep-dive tier (high conversion among the WC3-nostalgia core), or the BTD6-style review-channel tier (volume, mainstream TD audience)? Each implies different game-state readiness at launch.

2. **The Roblox-TD elephant.** Roblox TD is arguably the genuine living inheritor of the WC3 custom-map mass-modding ecosystem, even if it's outside our cultural reference frame. Do we want to research whether any cross-pollination is possible (modding posture, creator-economy hooks, lobby UX patterns), or do we treat Roblox as a wholly different audience and ignore?

3. **"Solved meta" vs. mode-breadth as anti-rote answer.** Creators converge on roguelite/randomization as the anti-solved-meta lever. Ash to Altar's bet is that *seven mode-types* is the lever instead. Is the PM willing to commit to this distinction explicitly in marketing — i.e., "we beat solved-meta with breadth, not with random" — or do we want to also reserve a roguelite-flavored mode within the seven?

4. **The "lobby UX is the breakage point" lesson from SC2 Arcade.** SC2 had the more powerful editor and still lost the cultural slot because lobby-discovery rotted. What's the minimum viable lobby/discovery UX we can ship at launch (Promise 2) that doesn't replicate SC2's rot? Is this a Phase-4 architecture question we need to pre-empt now?

5. **Element TD 2 / Legion TD 2 as friendly precedent or competitive shadow?** They occupy adjacent mode-slots in the lineage AtoA wants to cover comprehensively. Do we treat them as audience-warming proof-of-demand (positive), or as audience-fragmenting incumbents that we now have to *outdo per mode* rather than just *cover*? This may reshape Promise 1's polish-bar definition.
