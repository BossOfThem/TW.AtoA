# SYNTHESIS — AtoA vs. MtM Product-Shape Decision

**Date:** 2026-04-28
**Swarm rounds:** R1 (12 agents) + R2 (4) + R3 (5) + R4 (2 cycles) + 3 alignment-checks
**Status:** Final swarm output — feeds back into `decisions/2026-05-07-atoa-shippable-mtm-successor-pivot.md`

---

## TL;DR

The swarm converges on **paid Early Access single-product, at $15-20, scope-cut to a 2-of-7 mode-types feature-complete commitment** (most defensibly Tower + PvP-Co-op), with the remaining five mode-types framed as roadmap stretch. The scope-cut is the precondition; the path is the operating mechanism. The single most important conditional: this recommendation rests on the seven-mode promise being editable; if Promise 1's full scope is held verbatim, neither paid-EA *nor* the proposed two-product pivot is reachable, and the path debate becomes decorative until the scope is resolved upstream.

## What the swarm converged on (high-confidence findings)

- **Reachability inversion (Promise-1 swarmer + R3 turn-in §2).** Across the surveyed population of solo-dev shippers — Caves of Qud (15+ years, one mode-type), Dwarf Fortress (20+ years, one mode-type, art outsourced), Cogmind (4 person-years for one polished mode), Path of Achra (~3 years lower bound), Vampire Survivors (one-screen scope), Brotato, Dome Keeper — nobody ships seven mode-types. The closest "add one mode-type post-EA" cost (They Are Billions adding campaign) ran 18 small-team-months at compromised quality. Stormgate at $35-40M and ex-Blizzard RTS leadership failed to land exactly the AtoA mode-type spread. Battle Aces' May 2025 cancellation under Tencent backing at *narrow* scope is the flashing yellow on the floor. Promise 1 as written cannot be honored on either path; the scope question is load-bearing on the path question, not the reverse.
- **Lens convergence on paid-EA across genuinely different reasoning (alignment-check-3 §5; R3 turn-in §3).** PM-lens favors paid-EA on cash-flow timing, single-trunk focus, externalized scope discipline, marketing-surface coherence, lobby-substrate funding from day-one revenue, and risk-of-ruin profile. Player-lens favors paid-EA on storefront-narrative coherence, reference-class pattern-match (Legion TD 2 / Hades vs. Tower-Wars-2012 / DoA), price-as-trust-signal, friction-to-fun wall-clock, word-of-mouth recommendation shape, and durable progression substrate from day one. The convergence is from different lenses with different reasoning arriving at the same direction — not lens-collapse.
- **WC3→Dota2 pattern survives only under preconditions the pivot violates by construction (Cycle-1 Q2 §3).** The Promise-2 swarmer's Finding 10 — that the WC3→Dota2 lineage is the historically-validated funder pattern for lobby substrate — is the pivot's only structural advantage in R3. The pattern's portable causal mechanism (validation-cheap-substrate-then-engineered-successor) is real, but it requires (1) the substrate to be genuinely thin (terrain editor + workshop + replay only, no Tier-2 data/scripting) and (2) the successor to be perceived as a *distinct* commercial product, not "the same game with better graphics." The pivot's "MtM inherits AtoA verbatim, only Phase 5 is fresh" framing violates condition (2); AtoA's seven-mode promise at the free tier violates condition (1). Every successful enactment of the lineage in the evidence base also had platform-giant funders at both ends (Blizzard upstream, Valve/Riot downstream), and no solo-dev replication of both ends exists.
- **Monetization-discipline anti-alignment (Cycle-2 Q3 §3).** The pivot's commercial logic requires wishlist conversion to fund MtM; the pivot's player-experience story requires no MtM visibility in AtoA-free. These two requirements are in direct tension because conversion needs visibility. Under solo-dev runway pressure across the 12-36 month window, the probable failure mode is not IAP plumbing but wishlist-funnel architecture — a "Wishlist MtM" splash on AtoA-free's main menu — which is precisely the architecture Player-lens FINDING-3PL-7 reads as the player-experience-failure shape (the minute-30 reframing where AtoA-free is retroactively read as prototype-substrate). Paid-EA's discipline is roadmap-delivery (revenue and discipline are aligned); the pivot's discipline is anti-monetization (revenue and discipline are opposed). The temperaments are categorically different.
- **Risk-of-ruin asymmetry calibrated honestly (Cycle-2 Q4 §3).** The asymmetry is real but smaller than PM-lens FINDING-3PM-6 framed and larger than the pivot's defenders claim. Paid-EA's worst case is not the recoverable "60% Steam score, scope-cut and re-launch" scenario — it is closer to "no audience, no reviews, no signal, abandoned within 12 months." But the pivot's worst case is genuinely terminal in the career-capital sense: two products shipped, conditional revenue against a load-bearing wishlist-conversion bet with no positive solo-dev TW-genre precedent (PM-lens FINDING-3PM-10). The asymmetry tightens rather than drives the lens-convergence verdict.
- **Promise 3 is convergent at 5/5 across both paths under the same conditional (alignment-check-2 §5).** Both arguers self-scored Promise 3 at 5/5 via the same paid-base + day-1-visible-BP + cosmetic-only resolution to DA Reckoning #8's 1-08-23 vs 1-06-1 contradiction. Promise 3 is no longer the load-bearing discriminator. However, R3 Player-lens findings 3PL-3, 3PL-6, and 3PL-9 attack the pivot's Promise-3 architecture from the *experiential* angle Arguer A's structural defense did not address — the pivot satisfies Reckoning #8 structurally and violates it experientially via price-anchor reset and free-as-low-confidence corrosion.
- **Reconciled HTML-prototype number is materially below the headline (R1 turn-in; DA Reckoning #3).** The reconciled honest figure is ~50-55% deliverable-logic preserved + ~10-15% spec-blocked + ~30-35% rebuild — not the headline 65%. The math/balance/derivation work survives any substrate decision; only the rendering/transport/UI layer is path-specific. The "we already have AtoA half-built" sunk-cost framing for the pivot is materially weaker than the headline number suggests (FINDING-2B-07).

The PM has not surfaced this; the answer is private. — *Tagged: private-runway-dependent.*
- **PM temperament for paid-EA's public review-velocity scrutiny vs. patience for two-completion-event pivot (PM-lens §4 blind spots; Cycle-2 §3).** Both lenses flagged this as the question they could not adjudicate. - **Whether the pivot can be re-architected to honor the WC3→Dota2 lineage's preconditions (Cycle-1 §2 residual).** A re-architected pivot — thin substrate at AtoA-free (no data/scripting), genuine product-distinctness at MtM (different name, different art, different scope, no "same-game-better-graphics" framing) — could revive the pattern argument. This would be a meaningfully different decision than the one currently on the table. — *Tagged: pivot-redefinition-not-on-table.*

## The recommendation

The swarm recommends paid Early Access single-product as the AtoA delivery vehicle, scope-cut at written commitment to two feature-complete mode-types at 1.0 with the remaining five framed as roadmap stretch. The cut is the precondition; paid-EA's externalized refund-backed roadmap is the operating discipline mechanism that holds the cut commitment in public against revealed audience engagement. This composes Cycle-1's "Response A as precondition, Response B's mechanism layered on top" finding into a single product-shape: cut Promise 1 upstream via a dated `decisions/` entry under cascade discipline, then express the cut commitment as a paid-EA storefront page with public roadmap.

**Path:** Paid Early Access single-product at $15-20, built directly on the eventual engine substrate, with the prototype's preserved 50-55% deliverable-logic ported across rather than re-shipped as a free Steam SKU.

**Conditions (all must hold):**

1. A written, dated `decisions/` entry editing the north-star's Promise 1 from seven feature-complete mode-types at launch to a smaller initial-coverage commitment (the swarm's strongest single nomination is Tower + PvP-Co-op feature-complete at 1.0). The remaining five mode-types are explicitly tiered as roadmap stretch with no public completion date, framed at the storefront as Early Access development plan rather than launch promise.
2. A written, dated `decisions/` entry committing the project to never reverse the paid-base, day-1-visible-BP, cosmetic-only Promise-3 architecture across the EA→1.0 window. The Reckoning #8 conditional is honored *only* as long as the model never shifts toward F2P-cosmetics-live-service post-launch; the discipline is what makes Promise 3 cleanly resolvable.
3. The PM independently verifies — to themselves, since the swarm cannot — that personal runway and temperament for paid-EA's public review-velocity scrutiny over an 18-36 month EA window are honestly within reach. The swarm is explicit that this is a self-knowledge question, not a swarm-resolvable variable.

**The scope cut (Q1 Response A, the precondition):**

The swarm's R4 cycle-1 strongest single nomination is **Tower + PvP-Co-op feature-complete at 1.0**, with the five remaining mode-types (Hero / Horde / Custom / PVE-Campaign / Line) framed as Early Access roadmap stretch. The reasoning is that Tower has the densest existing demand evidence in the genre (R1 FINDING-1-02-1, FINDING-1-02-3) and PvP-Co-op is the Player-lens reference-class match to Legion TD 2 (FINDING-2WB-2, the closest direct comp at 87% positive across 6,350+ reviews and a 6-year track record). R4 explicitly noted residual uncertainty about which pair: Tower + Custom is coherent under Promise-2 substrate weighting, and the per-mode multipliers (3 civs, 9 Fusion Gods, 3 commanders) carry unbenchmarked cost that the Promise-1 swarmer flagged but did not isolate. The choice of pair is a PM design decision; the *existence* of a written cut is the precondition the swarm is naming.

**The discipline mechanism (Q1 Response B, the operating layer):**

Paid-EA's externalized refund-backed public roadmap operates the cut commitment in public against revealed audience engagement. Per PM-lens FINDING-3PM-5, the public roadmap is a written, dated, refund-backed contract with an audience that has paid in advance — every scope addition or removal must be either justified to that audience or rejected in public. This is the structural "no" surface that the pivot's HTML-iteration phase explicitly does not provide. Caves of Qud's nine-year EA arc is the positive reference: the team held an ambitious public commitment, let revenue and engagement determine what got engineered, cut piecewise in public, and shipped 1.0 with a substrate no pre-cut plan would have generated. The mechanism is structurally available under paid-EA and structurally unavailable under the pivot's free-product-with-internal-discipline shape.

## Why this recommendation (the load-bearing arguments)

- **Cash-flow timing is the largest single PM-economics discriminator and it strongly favors paid-EA (FINDING-3PM-1).** Paid-EA generates revenue from day one of the first commercial product at the ~20% wishlist→month-1-sale conversion median, with TD-adjacent solo paid-EA precedents at the $20K-$70K year-one revenue floor (FINDING-2WB-10). The pivot defers all revenue until MtM ships — empirically a multi-year gap whose bridge funding (personal savings) is the single most-documented modal indie failure mode in Agent 6's tombstone corpus.
- **The pivot's commercial mechanism is anti-aligned with its player-experience mechanism (Cycle-2 §3).** Wishlist conversion to MtM (commercial) requires MtM visibility in AtoA-free; preserving AtoA-free as a coherent destination (player-experience) requires no MtM visibility. These cannot both hold. Under runway pressure the player-experience side cracks predictably, and the architecture itself fails the discipline by requiring the conversion event the discipline forbids. Paid-EA's commercial mechanism (revenue from EA sales) is incentive-aligned with its discipline mechanism (deliver the public roadmap to keep selling).
- **The reference-class pattern-match for the target audience favors paid-EA decisively (Player-lens FINDING-3PL-2).** The TW-veteran whose priors include Wintermaul, SC2 Squadron TD, Tower Wars 2012, and Defenders of Ardania reads "free standalone TW on Steam, polish-ambitious, solo-dev" as the empty-of-survivors reference class. Paid-EA at $15-20 reads as Legion TD 2 / Hades / Manor Lords — the genre's living shipped survivors. Same player, same product, opposite priors triggered by price posture.
- **Lobby-substrate funding under any path is at indie/solo budgets always cut (Promise-2 swarmer §3).** Stormgate cut it at $37M; BAR cuts it on free/volunteer labor; Stardew deferred it eight years on paid-premium; Caves of Qud rolled it out piecewise across nine years. Paid-EA's day-one revenue is a funding mechanism that exists; the pivot's two-shot architecture is conditional on a wishlist-conversion bet with no positive solo-dev TW-genre precedent. The pivot's gesture toward the WC3→Dota2 historical pattern is real but not load-bearing because the pattern's solo-dev replication of both ends is unprecedented.
- **Storefront-narrative coherence is the foundation of every promise the project makes (Player-lens FINDING-3PL-1).** Paid-EA's page tells one story: this is unfinished, here is what works today, here is the roadmap, you pay $15 to ride along. The pivot's two-product storefront-pair tells two contradictory stories — AtoA is free and complete enough to play forever *and* MtM is the real version you should buy — and the player resolves the contradiction by either depressing AtoA's player base (waiting for MtM) or depressing MtM's conversion (AtoA was enough). The architecture forces a contradiction the dev has not resolved.
- **Risk-of-ruin asymmetry tightens rather than drives the conclusion (Cycle-2 §3).** Both paths have substantial terminal-failure probability mass at solo-dev scale. Paid-EA's terminal failure produces a public negative-review track-record on the dev account; the pivot's terminal failure forecloses the next attempt because the load-bearing wishlist-conversion bet's failure leaves the dev with two completed products and no career-capital runway. The asymmetry is incremental weight on a verdict the lens convergence already produced.
- **Scope-discipline externalization is the decisive Promise-1 reachability mechanism (PM-lens FINDING-3PM-5; Cycle-1 §1).** Promise 1's seven-mode commitment is the single largest scope-discipline question in the project. Paid-EA's public refund-backed roadmap is structurally a better scope-cutting instrument than the pivot's HTML-iteration internal discipline, because the cut is made against revealed audience priorities rather than predicted ones. Caves of Qud is the positive reference for this exact mechanism.

## Why NOT the original pivot (with care)

The PM proposed the pivot. Honoring that proposal honestly requires being precise about what the swarm found rather than restating the convergence as if it were obvious.

- **The reachability gap is unchanged by product-shape.** The Promise-1 swarmer's verdict applies to both paths: nobody in the surveyed solo-dev population ships seven mode-types, and Stormgate at $40M with exactly the AtoA mode-spread failed. The pivot does not lower the polish bar; it doubles the completion events at two polish bars. The headline 65% HTML-preserved figure was reconciled to ~50-55% deliverable + 10-15% spec-blocked + 30-35% rebuild, and the sunk-cost framing of the pivot is materially weaker than the original number implies because the math/balance work transfers across substrates.
- **Monetization discipline is structurally anti-aligned with the pivot's commercial logic (Cycle-2 Q3 §3).** This is the load-bearing finding neither R2 arguer fully named. Wishlist conversion needs MtM visibility; player-experience integrity needs MtM invisibility. The pivot's strongest-version-of-itself reading (no MtM marketing during AtoA's lifecycle, AtoA-as-genuine-destination) is a charitable pivot the architecture cannot afford under solo-dev runway pressure. The probable pivot is the wishlist-funnel pivot that fails at the minute-30 reframing Player-lens predicted.
- **Lens convergence on paid-EA is from different reasoning across genuinely different lenses (alignment-check-3 §5).** The convergence is not lens-collapse. PM-lens reads cash-flow timing; Player-lens reads storefront coherence; Promise-1 swarmer is path-agnostic but neutral-to-favorable on paid-EA's externalized scope-discipline; Promise-2 swarmer is the lone lens that gives the pivot a structural argument and concedes the argument's solo-dev replication is unprecedented. The convergence is genuine signal, not echo.
- **The WC3→Dota2 analogy doesn't survive its own preconditions (Cycle-1 Q2 §3).** The pivot reaches for the lineage as the historical funder of Promise 2's substrate. The pattern is real but its portability requires a thin substrate (which AtoA's seven-mode promise violates) and a distinct commercial successor (which "MtM inherits AtoA verbatim" violates). Every successful enactment had platform-giant funders at both ends. The analogy is the *generous* analogy the pivot reaches for and the *unmatched* analogy the evidence supplies.
- **Player-lens narrative damage from price-anchor reset is the under-examined vector (Player-lens FINDING-3PL-9).** Once a player plays AtoA-free, MtM at $20-30 reads as "I'm being charged for what I already had" — the exact PvZ2 international and Underlords S2 anger pattern. Paid-EA at $15-20 day-one anchors the IP at fair price; 1.0 at $20-25 reads as "fair price for the finished version." Same dollars, totally different felt deal. The pivot inadvertently engineers the worst possible price anchor for MtM.

## Reversal conditions

- **The PM personally verifies multi-year savings runway and temperament for an anti-monetization free-product discipline under deferred-revenue pressure.** Cycle-2 Q3 explicitly named this as the residual variable that, if favorable, weakens the anti-alignment finding. A PM with three-plus years of personal runway and a stated tolerance for free-product-with-no-monetization-plumbing through MtM completion can rehabilitate the pivot's discipline question. The swarm cannot measure this; the PM can.
- **The pivot is re-architected to honor the WC3→Dota2 lineage's two structural conditions (Cycle-1 Q2 §3 residual).** A thin AtoA-free substrate (no Tier-2 data/scripting), with MtM explicitly framed as a graphical-engine-replication for fans rather than as the same-game-with-better-graphics, with a distinct name / different art / smaller scope, would revive the lineage argument. This is a meaningfully different pivot than the one currently on the table.
- **Independent evidence emerges of solo-dev free-then-premium Steam-to-Steam wishlist conversion at rates that fund engine work in the TW genre.** Swarmer A's honest-negative section explicitly stated this evidence does not exist in the searched record. New positive evidence — a published postmortem, a developer interview with concrete conversion numbers, a recent solo case study — would weaken Cycle-2 Q4's terminal-failure-mode framing for the pivot.
- **The PM honestly weights the WC3→Dota2 historical pattern argument (Promise-2 swarmer Finding 10) more heavily than the lens convergence on paid-EA.** The pattern is real and the swarm conceded its causal mechanism is portable in principle; the question is whether the PM's priors weight the historical analogy higher than the empirical absence of solo-dev replication. Both readings are coherent. The swarm's recommendation is that the empirical absence dominates; a PM whose priors invert that weighting reaches a different conclusion legitimately.

## Confidence calibration

The swarm's overall confidence in the recommendation is medium-high, with sharp variance across dimensions. Confidence is high on the lens-convergence finding (PM-lens, Player-lens, and Promise-2 swarmer producing different content via different reasoning that lands in the same direction) and on the reachability inversion (Promise-1 swarmer's evidence base is strong and uncontested across R3 and R4). Confidence is high that the pivot's commercial-mechanism / player-experience-mechanism anti-alignment is real and load-bearing, because the structure of the tension is logical rather than empirical. Confidence is medium on the specific mode-type pair recommended (Tower + PvP-Co-op vs. Tower + Custom is genuinely unresolved). Confidence is low on the variables the swarm explicitly cannot measure: PM personal runway, PM temperament under public-EA review-velocity scrutiny vs. anti-monetization deferred-revenue discipline, and design-quality-at-first-contact. The lens convergence and the reachability inversion are robust; the conditions under which the recommendation should be reversed are the conditions under which those low-confidence variables land far from the swarm's modal assumptions. The honest read is that the swarm has produced the strongest path-shape recommendation available given what it can measure, and has surfaced the dimensions on which that recommendation depends — the PM's job is to weight the unmeasured variables and decide whether the weighting tightens or loosens the verdict.
