## §1 — The 4 thesis axes (Loop 1, hard argument)

**Axis T1 — Identity-density at the unit/structure/tier substrate (Mechanic).** Per `r1-a §2 axis 2`, BTD6's 23 towers each carry bespoke 5-tier × 3-path identity ladders, but identity lives **at the tower** — there is no faction-tier overlay. ETD2 collapses identity into element-pair grammar (`r1-a §2 axis 2`: 6 elements → 59 towers, "identity emerges from element-pair grammar rather than per-tower bespoke design"). LTD2's identity is at the Legion (~10+) but per-fighter-stat (`r1-a §2 axis 1-2`). SquadTD shows the closest analogue (`r1-b §2 axis 2`: per-builder racial passive, ~9-12 builders × ~6-10 fighters). AtoA's locked 3-civ × 6-tower-tier × Fusion-recipe stack puts **three** authored identity layers (civ-hook + tower-role + fusion-product) on the same substrate; no comparator in the 8-set carries three load-bearing identity layers simultaneously.

**Axis T2 — Authored cross-coherence (Integration).** The cross-coherence audit's 36/36 finding (`SYNTHESIS.md`) shows every locked surface composes with every other locked surface without contradiction — Fusion respects attack-type RPS, civ-hooks respect tier-up cost grammar, paid-base respects day-1-BP cosmetic-only lock. None of the comparator R1 cells evidence an audited cross-system coherence claim of comparable scope; BTD6's Knowledge-tree + Hero + Paragon stack (`r1-a §2 axis 4`) is broad but un-audited for cross-surface contradiction. This is internal-discipline-flavoured (Charter §1 secondary), but it is the substrate that lets T1 and T3 hold up under play.

**Axis T3 — Mechanic novelty: Fusion 9-recipe lock + 7-type attack-type RPS (Mechanic).** ETD2's element-pair recombination (`r1-a §2 axis 3`) is the closest comparator to Fusion grammar but is **emergent-combinatorial** (any pair → tower) rather than **authored-9-lock**. SquadTD's recombinations are emergent build-orders (`r1-b §1`: "no native fusion-recipe lock; recombinations are emergent build-orders rather than authored hard-locks"). The 7-type attack/armor RPS layered atop Fusion is a deliberate authored grammar; no comparator R1 cell describes a comparable attack-type lattice.

**Axis T4 — Scope-fit: depth-per-dollar at solo-paid-EA shape (Scope-fit).** Path of Achra (`r1-c §1, §2 axis 9`: 1 mode, 98% positive, ~3K reviews, $9.99, single dev) is the bucket's existence-proof that 1-mode-at-depth ships successfully at solo scale. AtoA's post-supersession Tower + PvP-Co-op cut (decisions/2026-04-29-promise-1-scope-cut.md) is a **two-mode-at-depth** posture — strictly more scope-ambitious than Achra's single-mode existence-proof while still inside the proven solo/tiny-team band, and below the BTD6/Mindustry ceiling that requires a decade of live-service or open-source community to reach. Paired with paid-base + day-1-BP + cosmetic-only (decisions/2026-04-29-promise-3-paid-base-discipline-lock.md), AtoA sits **norm-conformant** with the LTD2/Achra/Mindustry/TaB cosmetic-only cluster (`alignment-check §4 obs 2`) — meaning the no-P2W lock is not the differentiator, the **depth-stack on top of it** is.

## §3 — Loop 3 synthesis (R2-B-anticipated rebuttals defended)

**Load-bearing 3 axes for the differentiation paragraph: T1 (identity-density), T3 (mechanic novelty), T4 (scope-fit). T2 (cross-coherence) is supporting evidence, not headline.**

*Anticipated R2-B rebuttal on T3:* "Authored-lock is restrictive vs ETD2's emergent grammar — players will prefer combinatorial freedom." **Defense:** authored-9-lock is the *design choice* that keeps Fusion balanceable under solo-team economy-tuning bandwidth (per `decisions/§4.6` paper-balance ratification). It is a scope-fit-coupled mechanic — the lock *is* the differentiator vs ETD2's 59-emergent-tower combinatorial space, not a deficit.

*Anticipated R2-B rebuttal on T4:* "Tower + PvP-Co-op is two modes; TaB took 18 months to add one mode (`r1-c §2 axis 9`) — AtoA cannot ship two modes at the depth claimed." **Defense:** AtoA's two modes share a single substrate (Tower-defense substrate × PvP-Co-op multiplayer skin) more tightly than TaB's Survival ↔ Campaign which required new RPG-Empire-metalayer authoring (`r1-c §2 axis 4`). *Anticipated R2-B rebuttal on T2:* conceded — T2 is supporting, not headline. It buttresses T1/T3 ("the three identity layers don't contradict; the Fusion grammar respects attack-type RPS") but does not lead the paragraph.
