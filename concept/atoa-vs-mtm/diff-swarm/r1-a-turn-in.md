## §3 — Load-bearing finding

This triad teaches three things about the comparator landscape AtoA enters. **First**, the Steam-review weight distribution is brutally lopsided: BTD6 (~225k @ 97%) sits on roughly two orders of magnitude more review evidence than ETD2 (~1.6k @ 90%) and an order of magnitude more than LTD2 (~14k @ 87%). The "review-anchor" the player-lens analysis treats as reachable is the LTD2 / ETD2 band, not the BTD6 band — BTD6 is the polish-and-content asymptote that ten-year live-service buys, not a near-term reachable target. **Second**, none of the three comparators ship a Workshop / scripting / map-editor surface; the WC3-lineage triad has decisively *not* ported the modding substrate forward into standalone form, even though all three trace lineage to a modding ecosystem. The "WC3 Custom Map heritage" framing is therefore about *play patterns inherited* (send-creep PvP, fighter-roster Legion grammar, element-combo towers) rather than *modding tools inherited*. **Third**, monetization-posture stratifies cleanly: LTD2 and ETD2 hold a strict cosmetic-only / paid-DLC line with no live-service IAP funnel, while BTD6 layers consumable Powers and Trophy Store atop free updates and draws explicit P2W critique despite its review ceiling — meaning the cosmetic-only posture is the WC3-lineage *norm*, not a differentiation move. The genuine breadth-vs-depth contrast inside this bucket is BTD6's wide-roster live-service vs. LTD2/ETD2's narrow-roster competitive-PvP focus; both shapes have shipped successfully at the price-points sampled.
