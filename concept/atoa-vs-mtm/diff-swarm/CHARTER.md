## §1 — Question framing

**Top-level question:** *"On what specific axes does AtoA do better than the closest comparators in the tower-wars-and-adjacent design space, and on what specific axes do those comparators do better than AtoA?"*

**"Better" decomposes into four candidate axes** per PREP §3 framing — committed to **(2)–(3) as primary** and **(1)–(4) as secondary**:

1. **Polish** — execution quality at first contact (e.g. Stormgate's failure mode). *Secondary because unmeasurable pre-engine.*
2. **Mechanic** — load-bearing system design that is genuinely novel or non-trivially recombined (e.g. Fusion 9-recipe lock, attack-type 7-type RPS, per-civ identity-hook). *Primary.*
3. **Scope-fit** — depth-per-dollar vs comparator at the post-cut Tower + PvP-Co-op shape (or Tower + Custom under PM-Fork-2 redirect). *Primary.*
4. **Integration** — how locked surfaces compose with each other (the cross-coherence audit's 36/36 finding is a real edge). *Secondary because internal-discipline-flavoured rather than player-felt.*

## §3 — Axes of comparison (9 axes)

Per PREP §3 list, 9 axes × 8 comparators = **72-cell matrix**:

1. **Units** — playable mobile-unit roster size, role distribution, control surface.
2. **Structures** — tower roster size, tier ladder shape, identity-density per civ.
3. **Tiers** — tier-progression depth, tier-up cost grammar, tier-up payoff curve.
4. **Progression** — commander leveling, Fusion-equivalent or other meta-progression, Tribute-equivalent currency layers.
5. **Lobby / multiplayer** — lobby-substrate breadth, custom-game support, matchmaking, observer mode.
6. **Monetization** — base price, IAP shape, BP cadence, cosmetic-only or P2W posture.
7. **Replay / observer** — replay format, in-game observer, post-match analytics.
8. **Modding / custom-game surface** — map editor, scripting layer, asset-import pipeline, workshop.
9. **Mode-roster breadth-vs-depth ratio** — number of mode-types shipped × depth-per-mode (a critical axis for the post-Promise-1-cut framing).

## §4 — Swarm shape

**Round 1 (3 parallel agents):**
- **R1-A:** Legion TD 2 + Bloons TD 6 + Element TD 2 — the WC3-lineage-and-polish-anchor triad.
- **R1-B:** SC2 Squadron TD + Tower Defense Simulator (Roblox) — the Map-Maker-and-F2P-lobby-discoverability pair.
- **R1-C:** Mindustry + Path of Achra + They Are Billions — the substrate-modding-and-solo-dev-and-RTS-hybrid triad.

Each R1 agent produces:
- A 9-row × N-col matrix where N = comparators in their bucket (3 / 2 / 3).
- One "key data points" callout per comparator (review count, price, mode-roster size, modding presence, etc.).
- One "evidence-quality" honest-confidence note per cell (web-validated / inference-from-reputation / unverifiable).

**Alignment-check between R1 and R2.** Cross-bucket consistency verification (same axes → same depth of analysis); flag drift.

**Round 2 (2 parallel dialecticians):**
- **R2-A:** Differentiation-thesis dialectician — argues for the 3-5 axes where AtoA does better. Required to cite specific comparator cells from R1 matrices.
- **R2-B:** Counter-thesis dialectician — argues for the 2-3 axes where comparators do better than AtoA. Required to cite specific comparator cells from R1 matrices.

**Round 3 (1 synthesizer):**
- Combines R2-A and R2-B into a load-bearing differentiation paragraph. Names "we are not trying to win on these" axes explicitly. Outputs the differentiation-thesis decision file.

## §6 — Context discipline

The differentiation swarm must respect the locked design substrate per PROJECT-ARC.md §3 cascade-respect block. The differentiation thesis describes what AtoA *is*, not what it *should become*. If a comparator dominates AtoA on a load-bearing axis (e.g. modding surface), that finding feeds Phase F Steam-page framing as "we are not trying to win on this" rather than triggering a redesign of the locked substrate. Reversal trigger RT-3 (PROJECT-ARC.md §8) names the narrow circumstance under which a comparator finding could trigger upstream rebuild — load-bearing dominance + audience-priority confirmation.
