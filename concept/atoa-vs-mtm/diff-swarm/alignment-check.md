## §4 — Cross-bucket load-bearing observations (5 named)

1. **Modding-surface dominance is a single-comparator anomaly, not a genre norm.** Across all 8 comparators, only **Mindustry** ships first-party modding + Workshop + scripting (R1-C axis 8); SquadTD's editor-power is the *host platform's* (Galaxy) not the product's; the WC3-lineage triad (LTD2/BTD6/ETD2) ships zero modding surface despite tracing modding lineage. AtoA conceding modding to Mindustry concedes to **one** comparator, not the field.

2. **Cosmetic-only / no-P2W is the WC3-lineage and indie norm; live-service P2W is the F2P-treadmill norm.** LTD2, ETD2, Mindustry, Achra, TaB all hold strict cosmetic-only-or-paid-DLC lines. BTD6 (consumable Powers) and TDS (Golden Perks stat-buff path) are the soft-P2W vectors — both are live-service / F2P shapes. AtoA's no-P2W lock is **norm-conformant** within the paid-EA cluster, not a differentiator within it.

3. **Post-EA mode-add cost is empirically expensive (TaB ~18 months for a small team).** The single most cite-worthy datum across all three buckets for the supersession's "breadth is unreachable" framing. R2-B's counter-thesis on mode-roster breadth-deficit should anchor here.

4. **Steam-review weight is brutally lopsided across the comparator set.** BTD6 ~225k @ 97% (R1-A) and Mindustry ~25k @ 96% (R1-C) sit one-to-two orders of magnitude above LTD2 (~14k), TaB (~18.7k @ 80%), Achra (~3k @ 98%), ETD2 (~1.6k @ 90%). The reachable review-anchor band for a paid-EA solo/tiny-team launch is the LTD2 / Achra / ETD2 1k-15k band, not the BTD6 / Mindustry ceiling.

5. **Depth-over-breadth is a proven solo / small-team success path.** Path of Achra (1 mode, 98% positive, ~3k reviews, $9.99, single dev) is the cleanest existence-proof; LTD2 (narrow-roster competitive-PvP, 87% positive) is a second proof at the multiplayer-substrate end. The "1-2 mode-types at depth" posture has shipped successfully across both single-player and multiplayer ends of the comparator set.
