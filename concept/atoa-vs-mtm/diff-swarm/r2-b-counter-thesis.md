## §1 — The three counter-thesis axes

### Counter-axis C1 — Modding / custom-game surface (Mindustry dominates)

**R1 evidence.** Per `r1-c §2 axis 8`, Mindustry ships first-party Steam Workshop integration, native Java/JSON content modding, an open-source codebase, a bundled map editor, and **Mindustry Logic** — an in-game programmable processor scripting layer. Per `r1-b §2 axis 8`, SquadTD inherits Galaxy Editor power but only as host-platform substrate (not the product's own surface). Per `r1-a §2 axis 8`, **none** of LTD2/BTD6/ETD2 ship any first-party modding surface despite WC3-mod lineage. Mindustry is the bucket's modding ceiling — and per alignment-check §4 finding #1, modding-dominance is a **single-comparator anomaly**, not a genre norm.

**Decomposition (PREP §3).** Mechanic: a player-programmable scripting layer is structurally absent from AtoA's locked substrate ([`CONCEPT.md`](../../../CONCEPT.md) §§4 / 5.4). Scope-fit: open-source + Workshop + scripting requires explicit engine-time investment a solo/tiny-team paid-EA shop cannot fund on the post-cut roadmap (per [`decisions/2026-04-29-promise-1-scope-cut.md`](../../../decisions/2026-04-29-promise-1-scope-cut.md)). Integration: nothing in the audited 36/36 cross-coherence stack composes with a modding surface — adding one is greenfield, not a tweak.

**RT-3 disposition.** Concession, not reversal. Per CHARTER §6, RT-3 only fires on load-bearing dominance **AND** audience-priority confirmation; the audience for the post-cut Tower + PvP-Co-op shape is the LTD2 / Achra / ETD2 review-band reachable cluster (alignment-check §4 finding #4) for whom modding is not the buy-signal. Frame as Steam-page honest concession.

### Counter-axis C2 — Mode-roster breadth (BTD6 + TDS dominate)

**R1 evidence.** Per `r1-a §2 axis 9`, BTD6 ships Standard difficulties + Boss + Race + Odyssey + Contested Territory + Co-op + Sandbox at deep polish per mode — the genre's breadth-and-depth outlier on ten-year live-service investment. Per `r1-b §2 axis 9`, TDS ships ~6-8 mode-types active (Standard / Molten / Fallen / Hardcore / Polluted Wasteland II + rotating events). The empirical cost-anchor for an AtoA-shaped team to add even one extra mode-type post-EA is per `r1-c §2 axis 9` + `r1-c §1` They Are Billions: **~18 months elapsed from EA-survival to 1.0-campaign**, with publicly extended timeline mid-build, from a small team with shipping experience.

**Decomposition (PREP §3).** Scope-fit: the post-cut shape is Tower + PvP-Co-op feature-complete at 1.0 (decision 2026-04-29); five non-launch mode-types live in roadmap-stretch language. Mechanic: AtoA's substrate richness lives in cross-coherence not mode-count. Integration: each additional mode-type re-opens economy / fusion / attack-type tuning across 36 already-audited compositions.

**RT-3 disposition.** Concession. The TaB 18-month datum is the load-bearing breadth-cost anchor (alignment-check §4 finding #3) and substantiates the scope-cut's "breadth is unreachable" framing rather than reversing it. Frame as Steam-page honest concession; depth-over-breadth (`r1-c §3` Path of Achra existence-proof) is the chosen counter-posture.

### Counter-axis C3 — Discoverability substrate (TDS Roblox front-page; SquadTD Arcade-tab inheritance)

**R1 evidence.** Per `r1-b §1` + `§2 axis 5`, TDS sits on **4.65 billion+ visits / 3.3M favorites** via Roblox front-page algorithmic surfacing — discoverability that an indie Steam launch structurally cannot match. Per `r1-b §2 axis 5`, SquadTD inherits SC2 Arcade-tab as its discovery surface (a closed pipe AtoA cannot enter). Per `r1-a §3` + alignment-check §4 finding #4, the BTD6 ~225k-review polish-and-content asymptote is itself partly a discoverability artifact of ten-year live-service.

**Decomposition (PREP §3).** Scope-fit: AtoA must earn its own Steam-page surface from cold start — no inherited platform-tab. Mechanic / Integration: irrelevant to discoverability — this is a marketing-substrate axis, not a design-substrate one.

**RT-3 disposition.** Concession. Per alignment-check §4 finding #2, TDS's discoverability advantage is paired with a mild-P2W Golden-Perk vector AtoA's no-P2W lock explicitly refuses; AtoA cannot import the substrate without importing the monetization shape. Frame as Steam-page honest concession via the cosmetic-only / no-P2W / paid-EA cluster posture.

## §2 — Loop-2 weakest-claim flag (for R2-A awareness)

The **weakest** of the three is **C3 (discoverability)**. It is genuinely a concession axis but it is *not a design-substrate finding* — it is a marketing-substrate one. R2-A could legitimately push back that conceding "discoverability" is conceding to platform mechanics rather than to comparator design, and therefore mis-categorizes against the four PREP §3 "better" decomposition axes (Polish / Mechanic / Scope-fit / Integration) — none of which discoverability cleanly maps to. C1 (modding) and C2 (mode-roster breadth) decompose cleanly into Scope-fit + Integration (and C1 also Mechanic); C3 mostly only touches Scope-fit by analogy. **Flag for R2-A:** if R2-A wants to challenge one of my three, C3 is the soft target. C1 and C2 are robust against challenge given the Mindustry single-comparator-anomaly framing and the TaB 18-month numerical anchor respectively.

A secondary weakness on C2: the "depth-over-breadth is a proven path" rebuttal (Achra existence-proof) is so strong that C2 risks being read as *not actually a concession* — which is why the framing must be careful. AtoA still ships **fewer mode-types** than BTD6 / TDS by an order of magnitude; the concession is real even if the chosen counter-posture is defensible.

## §3 — Loop-3 synthesis with concession framing

The differentiation thesis must be honest about three axes where the comparator field beats AtoA, framed not as fix-targets but as deliberate non-pursuits the Steam page should name explicitly. **C1 — modding surface:** Mindustry's open-source + Workshop + Logic-scripting stack is the genre's modding ceiling and concedes to one comparator only; AtoA does not ship a modding surface and the Steam page should say so plainly ("AtoA is not a modding platform — if you want player-programmable factory logic, play Mindustry"). **C2 — mode-roster breadth:** BTD6 ships seven-plus mode-types and TDS ships six-to-eight; AtoA's post-cut shape is Tower + PvP-Co-op feature-complete with five non-launch mode-types in roadmap-stretch, and the TaB 18-month EA→1.0 mode-add datum is the empirical anchor for why a tiny team cannot match that breadth at launch — the Steam page should name the depth-over-breadth posture using Path of Achra's existence-proof as the precedent ("two-mode-types-at-depth, not eight-modes-at-breadth"). **C3 — discoverability substrate:** TDS's Roblox-front-page surface and SquadTD's SC2-Arcade-tab inheritance are platform-substrate advantages structurally unavailable to a Steam paid-EA launch; conceding here is conceding to the platform, not to a design choice, and the Steam-page framing is the cosmetic-only / no-P2W / paid-EA cluster posture rather than a defensive note.

Per CHARTER §6, none of C1 / C2 / C3 trips RT-3: C1 is single-comparator dominance without audience-priority confirmation, C2's breadth-cost is empirically affirmed not contradicted, C3 is platform-substrate not design-substrate. All three feed Phase F Steam-page draft (PROJECT-ARC.md §5 work item F.2) as "what we will not do" honesty rather than upstream redesign signal.
