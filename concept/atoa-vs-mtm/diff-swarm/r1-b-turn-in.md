## Key data points

**SC2 Squadron TD (SquadTD).** Free SC2 Arcade map (originally released ~2011, descended in concept-lineage from WC3 Legion TD circa 2009); 1-8 players, two teams defending a Security System across 31+ creep waves; ~9-12 named "builders" (Beast, Elemental, Ghost, Shadow, Ancient, Celestial, Mechanical, Nature, Automaton, Human/Custom etc.) with per-builder racial passives (Ghost = Ethereal Cloaking 7.5% evade); core round-grammar = build static towers in build phase, towers morph to AI-controlled fighters in combat phase; "Arcade Mode" unlocks all builders to bypass progression grind. Hosted inside SC2 client; lobby-discoverability gated by SC2 Arcade browser. No native fusion-recipe lock; recombinations are emergent build-orders rather than authored hard-locks. No price; ad-hoc Discord community; no Steam-comparable review surface.

**Tower Defense Simulator (TDS, Roblox).** Paradoxum Games, released June 2019; 4.65 billion+ visits / 3.3M favorites cumulative as of early 2026; F2P with Robux IAP layer (VIP gamepass, VIP+ $4.99/mo subscription, Premium/Deluxe/Golden Skincrates, Battlepass Premium tier 499 Robux with paid level-skip starting 32 Robux/level); ~30+ towers across rarity tiers (Saboteur added April 2026); modes include Standard, Molten, Fallen, Hardcore (gated at L50, gem-currency, +50% costs, 0% shared cash), Polluted Wasteland II (L50, 25-wave high-stat boss-dense), plus rotating limited-time events (Operation I.C.E., Hexscape, Spring Frenzy / Ducky Revenge). Coop-focused; lobby-substrate is Roblox front-page discoverability + in-game matchmaking; cosmetic-skin ladder includes Golden Perks that buff base stats (mild P2W vector via grind-or-pay 50,000 Coins or Robux-skincrate path).

## Load-bearing finding

This bucket teaches AtoA two distinct lessons by sitting on opposite ends of the polish-substrate axis. SquadTD shows that a *mod-substrate* TD can sustain a community for 15+ years on the strength of per-builder identity-density and a single deep ruleset, with no monetization and no mode-breadth — but its discoverability is gated entirely by the host platform's Arcade tab and its surface is invisible to anyone not already inside SC2. AtoA's 3-civ × 6-tower × Fusion stack is structurally richer than any single SquadTD builder, but AtoA cannot inherit SquadTD's Arcade-tab discoverability and must earn its own Steam-page surface; the depth-over-breadth posture (Tower + PvP-Co-op feature-complete at 1.0) is the right comparator-mirror. TDS shows the opposite extreme: F2P lobby-discoverability via Roblox's algorithmic front-page is a genuine accelerant (4.65B visits) but the cost of that surface is a multi-layer monetization treadmill including a mild-P2W Golden-Perk vector — a posture AtoA's solo-first / cosmetic-only / no-P2W locks explicitly refuse. The honest takeaway: AtoA cannot win on discoverability-substrate (no Arcade tab, no Roblox algorithm) and cannot win on mode-roster breadth (TDS ships ~6-8 modes via live-service treadmill); AtoA's edge has to come from authored cross-coherence (Fusion 9-recipe lock, attack-type 7-RPS, per-civ identity-hook) at a polish-bar SquadTD's mod-substrate cannot reach and at a monetization-cleanliness TDS's Robux-economy cannot offer. Map-Maker-substrate dominance and F2P-lobby-discoverability dominance are explicit "we are not trying to win on these" axes for the differentiation thesis.
