The convergence: PM-lens, Player-lens, and Promise-2 swarmer all read paid-EA single-product as the cleaner shape on the live differentiators — cash-flow timing, marketing-surface coherence, scope-discipline externalization, first-contact narrative, reference-class pattern-match, and substrate funding mechanism. The destabilizer: the Promise-1 swarmer, reading the entire successful-solo-dev population for multi-mode-type shippers, found base rate effectively zero on either path, with the ceiling reference (Stormgate at $40M with ex-Blizzard RTS leads) failing to deliver the same mode-type spread Promise 1 commits to. The inversion vs. R2: where Arguer A and Arguer B both self-scored Promise 1 at 4/5 — Arguer A trusting that HTML iteration economics make seven mode-types tractable at solo scope, Arguer B trusting that paid-EA's phased-delivery convention can carry the commitment across the EA→1.0 window — R3's Promise-1 swarmer surfaces that *neither* assumption has positive empirical precedent and that the closest data points (Caves of Qud 15+ years, Cogmind 4+ years for one mode-type, They Are Billions 18 small-team-months for *one additional* mode-type) imply Promise 1 as currently scoped is unreachable on either path without a written scope cut.

This is the coherence shape R3 produces: three lenses agreeing on path-shape, one lens (Promise-1 reachability) agreeing on a scope-cut precondition that applies regardless of path, and a fourth (Promise-2 substrate funding) supporting the pivot's WC3→Dota2 lineage logic on its own terms while conceding that the lineage's funder has historically been an upstream platform giant rather than the free product itself. R2's debate was about which of two paths is better; R3's surfacing is that the path question and the scope question are partially independent, and that the scope question is load-bearing in a way R2's self-scores did not register.

The R2-vs-R3 inversion most worth naming: alignment-check-2 explicitly told R3 to treat Promise-1 and Promise-2 as the live differentiators because Promise-3 had collapsed to a shared 5/5 across both arguers. R3 did this — and discovered Promise 1 is not actually a *path* differentiator at all on the reachability question. It is a *scope* differentiator that cuts orthogonally to the path debate. That changes what R4 has to close.

## 2. The reachability inversion — what Promise-1 swarmer's verdict does to the frame

The Promise-1 swarmer's finding is the single most consequential single-agent output of the entire swarm to date. The verdict, in compressed form: across the surveyed population of solo-dev success stories — Vampire Survivors, Brotato, Path of Achra, Dome Keeper, Cogmind, Caves of Qud, Dwarf Fortress — the consistent shape is one mode-type with depth/variety, three to fifteen-plus years to 1.0, art outsourced where present at all. Nobody in the searched record ships seven mode-types solo. The closest analog of "add one new mode-type post-EA" cost (They Are Billions adding campaign) ran 18 small-team-months at compromised quality. Naive multiplication for AtoA's six additional mode-types at solo scope produces 9 small-team-years or 18+ solo years. The ceiling reference (Stormgate, $35-40M, ex-Blizzard RTS leads, exactly AtoA's mode-type spread) failed to land. The Battle Aces cancellation (Tencent-backed, top RTS designer, *narrow* scope) is a flashing yellow even at zero-breadth.

This finding does not invalidate either path. It invalidates the assumption that *either path can deliver Promise 1 in its current frozen-north-star form at solo scope*. The implication is that the north-star statement, ratified at swarm launch and held verbatim across every round, contains a commitment that the swarm has now produced strong evidence cannot be honored on either path the swarm is debating. Two coherent responses are available, and R3 should not pick between them — that is a PM/R4 decision — but should name them precisely so R4 can close the fork.

**Response A: edit the north-star.** Reduce Promise 1 to a smaller scope that has positive solo-dev precedent. Plausible cuts include: ship two mode-types feature-complete at 1.0 (Tower + PvP-Co-op, the modes with strongest existing demand evidence per the Player-lens reference-class match), with the remaining five mode-types deferred to post-1.0, framed publicly as a roadmap, or cut entirely. Three civs and 9 Fusion Gods and 3 commanders are themselves multipliers on whichever mode-types ship; their reachability is also affected. Under Response A the path question becomes meaningful again — at scope-cut Promise 1, paid-EA's phased delivery is a strong fit and the pivot's two-shot architecture is a defensible alternative; R4 can adjudicate path normally.

**Response B: keep the north-star and accept that the project's commercial outcome will likely include scope failure.** This is the "commitment as discipline" reading: hold the seven-mode promise as the externalized "no" surface (per PM-lens FINDING-3PM-5), accept that some modes will ship thin, and let the public roadmap absorb the discipline cost. This response is structurally available under paid-EA (public roadmap is the contract) but harder to operationalize under the pivot (HTML iteration phase has no equivalent external commitment device, per the same finding). Under Response B the path question still meaningfully differentiates; the answer leans paid-EA on scope-discipline grounds.

Either response keeps the project tractable. Continuing without picking one is the failure mode the Promise-1 swarmer is naming. The honest read is that **the scope question is load-bearing on the path question**, not the other way around: at full scope, Promise 1 is unreachable and the path debate is decorative; at cut scope, the path debate is real and tractable. R3 cannot resolve this; R4 must.

## 3. The lens convergence — reconciling PM-lens, Player-lens, Promise-2 swarmer

PM-lens favors paid-EA on cash-flow timing, single-trunk focus, externalized scope discipline, marketing-surface coherence, lobby-substrate funding from day-one revenue, feature-completeness reachability (under EA→1.0 phased-delivery convention), and risk-of-ruin asymmetry (paid-EA's worst case is recoverable; the pivot's worst case is two products shipped with conditional revenue against a load-bearing wishlist-conversion bet that has no positive solo-dev TW-genre precedent).

Player-lens favors paid-EA decisively on six convergent player-felt asymmetries: storefront narrative coherence, reference-class pattern-match (Legion TD 2 / Hades vs. Tower-Wars-2012 / DoA), price-as-trust-signal alignment, friction-to-fun wall-clock from the player's lived perspective, word-of-mouth recommendation shape, and durable progression substrate from day one. The lens specifically surfaces that the strongest version of the pivot — discipline-perfect, AtoA-as-destination, no MtM marketing during AtoA's lifecycle — produces roughly equal player experience, but the *probable* version of the pivot (with wishlist-funnel architecture and MtM visibility, which Arguer A concession (a) names as the load-bearing assumption) produces materially worse player experience on every axis the lens reads.

Promise-2 swarmer cuts a different way. The five-tier decomposition (terrain editor, data/scripting layer, hosting/lobby infra, discovery/distribution, replay/observer) shows that *no* product shape at indie/solo budget has historically funded the full substrate at launch; the substrate is always either inherited (Unreal/Spring engine subsidy), outsourced to community loaders (SMAPI, tModLoader), deferred to long EA tails (Caves of Qud), or built by an upstream platform giant and exploited downstream (Blizzard→DotA→Valve→Dota 2; the WC3→Dota2 lineage Arguer A's pivot logic explicitly invokes). The honest read: the pivot's funding mechanism is *not* "the free product pays for the substrate" but rather "the free product validates the substrate cheaply enough that the paid successor can afford to finish it" — which is the WC3→Dota2 pattern, with AtoA-free playing the role of the WC3 custom map and MtM playing the role of the Valve-funded successor.

The reconciliation: Promise-2 swarmer gives the pivot a structural argument — the only one of the five lens/swarmer outputs that does — that paid-EA does not get. The argument is real: the historical funder of the lobby substrate has been the upstream platform, and the pivot is the only proposed shape that explicitly enacts the upstream/downstream pattern. Paid-EA forces the substrate to be funded from its own revenue, which the evidence base reads as resulting in deep cuts (Stormgate's terrain-editor-only at 1.0 with deeper editor slipped to undated post-launch milestones) or multi-year deferrals (Caves of Qud's 9-year piecewise rollout).

But the WC3→Dota2 argument has a load-bearing dependency the swarm cannot supply: every successful enactment of that pattern in the evidence base had a *funded upstream platform* (Blizzard built WC3's substrate at $0 marginal return because it was already the host of a $50 RTS) and a *funded downstream successor* (Valve hired IceFrog; Riot built TFT). AtoA's pivot proposes that the solo dev plays *both* roles — the upstream substrate-builder and the downstream substrate-finisher — funded only by the wishlist conversion between them. The pattern's historical track record is real; the solo-dev replication of both ends of it is unprecedented. Promise-2 swarmer's strongest finding (Finding 10) is also the finding that is hardest to transfer to AtoA's resource posture.

The synthesis: PM-lens and Player-lens favor paid-EA on the empirical and player-experiential axes they own, and the convergence is genuine (different lenses, same direction, different reasons). Promise-2 swarmer favors the pivot on the *historical pattern* argument but concedes the *resource posture* the pattern requires has never been replicated by a solo dev. The swarmers do not contradict; they describe different aspects of the same picture. The picture: paid-EA is the cleaner shape on what the swarm can measure; the pivot is the only shape that gestures at the historically-validated funding pattern for Promise 2's substrate, but the gesture is not load-bearing because the historical pattern requires resources the solo dev does not have.

## 4. Honest path scores after R3

Both arguers' R2 self-scores were Promise 1 = 4/5 on opposite paths. R3's Promise-1 swarmer surfaces that neither path's 4/5 was earned absent a written scope cut. The honest re-scores below reflect what R3 has actually produced as evidence, not what the arguers asserted in advocacy mode.

### Pivot (free AtoA → paid MtM)

- **Promise 1 — feature/polish/art-complete coverage of every TW mode-type at launch: 2/5.** Downgraded from Arguer A's self-scored 4/5. The Promise-1 swarmer's reachability verdict applies fully; HTML iteration economics (FINDING-2A-7) genuinely lower the per-mode design-validation cost but do not lower the polish-bar cost across seven mode-types, and Arguer A concession (e) ("no reference-class HTML game shipped seven mode-types at solo polish") is the held-back point that the Promise-1 swarmer's evidence base now confirms. Two completion events at two polish bars — the same scope problem, doubled.
- **Promise 2 — SC1/WC3 customizability + recognizability: 3/5.** Downgraded from Arguer A's 4/5. The WC3→Dota2 lineage argument (Promise-2 swarmer Finding 10) is the strongest case for the pivot in R3 and survives. But the lineage's historical funder is always upstream (Blizzard, Valve), not the solo dev playing both ends, and the wishlist-conversion bet (Arguer A concession (a), with no positive solo-dev TW-genre precedent per Swarmer-A honest-negative) is the operative variable. The pivot's two-shot architecture is the only shape that affords two operationalizations of substrate cost — but the second operationalization is conditional on the bet whose evidence base is empty.
- **Promise 3 — optional BP + Ranked layered without compromising regular play: 3/5.** Downgraded from Arguer A's 5/5. R2 alignment-check-2 noted both arguers' Promise-3 5/5 self-scores converged on the same paid-base + day-1-visible-BP + cosmetic-only conditional. R3 Player-lens FINDING-3PL-9 (price-anchor reset — once a player plays AtoA-free, MtM at $30 reads as "I'm being charged for what I already had") and FINDING-3PL-3 (free-as-low-confidence is corrosive on a day-1-visible BP) attack the pivot's Promise-3 architecture from angles Arguer A's structural defense did not address from the player's perceived experience. The pivot satisfies the Reckoning #8 conditional structurally; it violates it experientially.

### Paid-EA single-product

- **Promise 1 — feature/polish/art-complete coverage of every TW mode-type at launch: 2/5.** Downgraded from Arguer B's self-scored 4/5. Same Promise-1 reachability verdict. Paid-EA's phased-delivery convention (Hades, Manor Lords, Slay the Spire) is real but the Promise-1 swarmer's Finding 10 surfaces that EA convention is *depth-during-EA*, not *breadth-during-EA*; no precedent supports adding mode-types during EA. They Are Billions added one mode (campaign) in 18 small-team-months at compromised quality. Paid-EA externalizes scope discipline (PM-lens FINDING-3PM-5) more aggressively than the pivot, which is real value, but the discipline only helps if the dev is willing to publicly cut what the roadmap promised — which is the same discipline question that applies under Response B above.
- **Promise 2 — SC1/WC3 customizability + recognizability: 3/5.** Held at Arguer B's 4/5 minus one. Paid-EA funds substrate engineering from day-one revenue rather than deferring it to a hypothetical successor (Arguer B FINDING-2B-11), and the Player-lens hosting-moment finding (3PL-8) reads paid-EA as delivering the recognizability moment within the EA window rather than across a product boundary. But Promise-2 swarmer's evidence base shows paid-EA at indie scope historically *cuts* the substrate (Stormgate, Stardew, Caves of Qud's 9-year rollout) — paid-EA does not magic the substrate cost lower. Day-one revenue is a funding mechanism that exists, but it is not a sufficient one at the budgets the evidence describes.
- **Promise 3 — optional BP + Ranked layered without compromising regular play: 5/5.** Held at Arguer B's self-scored 5/5. The paid-base + day-1-visible-BP + cosmetic-only configuration is the configuration DA Reckoning #8 conceded resolves the 1-08-23 vs 1-06-1 contradiction cleanly, and paid-EA is the shape that natively expresses it without any audience commitment shifting between launch and 1.0. Player-lens findings 3PL-3, 3PL-6, 3PL-9 all align with paid-EA on this promise. This is the cleanest single promise-alignment outcome of either path.

### Aggregate read

At full north-star scope, both paths score Promise 1 at 2/5 and the path debate on Promise 1 is decorative. At a written scope cut (Response A), Promise 1 lifts on both paths; the path debate becomes a Promise 2 / Promise 3 / Promise-1-discipline question, and paid-EA's aggregate (2 / 3 / 5) edges the pivot's aggregate (2 / 3 / 3) on Promise 3 and ties on Promise 2 with different risk profiles. The pivot's only structural advantage in R3 is the Promise-2 swarmer's WC3→Dota2 historical pattern argument — and that argument's solo-dev replication is unprecedented.

## 5. R4 load-bearing questions

The forks below are what R4 must close. Each is convergence-detected (multiple R3 outputs surfaced it without resolving it) and load-bearing (the recommendation shape changes depending on the answer).

**Q1. Does Promise 1 hold at frozen-north-star scope, or is a written scope cut a precondition to either path being reachable?** This is the question the Promise-1 swarmer's verdict forces. R4 cannot honestly recommend a path without a written PM answer to whether the seven-mode commitment is held as discipline (Response B) or edited (Response A). If the answer is "edit the north-star," R4 needs to know to what scope. If the answer is "hold the commitment," R4 needs to know which path's scope-discipline mechanism (paid-EA's public roadmap vs. the pivot's HTML-iteration internal discipline) the PM trusts more.

**Q2. Is the WC3→Dota2 historical pattern (Promise-2 swarmer Finding 10) load-bearing enough to override the converging PM-lens and Player-lens preference for paid-EA?** The pivot's only structural advantage in R3 is this historical pattern argument. The pattern is real, but its historical funder has always been an upstream platform giant rather than the solo dev playing both ends. R4 needs the PM's honest weighting: does the gesture toward the pattern compensate for the empirical gap (no solo-dev replication of both ends), or is the empirical gap the dominant signal? Both readings are coherent.

**Q3. Would the dev's monetization discipline survive AtoA-free's week-12 install-curve flattening, or would the temptation to surface MtM in-product be the moment the pivot's player-experience story fails?** The Player-lens explicitly raised this as the question on which the pivot's player-experience story lives or dies. The strongest version of the pivot (no MtM marketing during AtoA's lifecycle) produces roughly equal player experience to paid-EA; the probable version (wishlist-funnel with MtM visibility) produces materially worse. R4 needs the PM's honest read of their own discipline under solo-dev career-capital pressure over 18+ months. This is a temperament question the PM-lens and Player-lens both flagged but neither could answer.

**Q4. What is the PM's honest weighting of cash-flow timing vs. soft-landing-pad value?** PM-lens FINDING-3PM-1 and FINDING-3PM-6 surface that the pivot's worst case (two products, conditional revenue against an empty-precedent bet) is structurally terminal while paid-EA's worst case (60% Steam score on EA) is recoverable through scope cuts and re-launch. But the pivot has a soft landing pad on the floor (free product cannot generate negative reviews that kill the product before it finds its audience) that paid-EA does not. R4 needs the PM to weight terminal-failure-mode probability against soft-landing-pad value at the PM's actual risk tolerance. Both readings are coherent and the answer determines which of the two "worst cases" should drive the recommendation.

The pivot's only structural advantage is the WC3→Dota2 historical pattern for Promise-2 substrate funding (Promise-2 swarmer Finding 10), and the pattern's solo-dev replication is unprecedented. If Promise-1 scope is not editable, neither path is reachable as written; the path debate is decorative until the scope question is answered. The R4 fork that matters most is Q1 (Promise-1 reachability and scope), because it determines whether Q2-Q4 are live decisions or moot. The honest conditional recommendation: paid-EA at written scope-cut Promise 1, with the scope cut itself being the load-bearing PM decision, and with R4 closing the temperament question (Q3) and the terminal-failure-vs-soft-landing-pad weighting (Q4) before any ratification.** R4 should refine; this is R3's best read, not the final answer.
