## 2. Re-scope vs. refine — honest call

The PM's hinted "rescope/pivot" framing has limited legs at the unit / structure / tier level. The locked surfaces — 9-recipe Fusion hard-lock, 3-civ-3-equation-form fingerprint, per-tower R1-R4 row authoring with 36/36 cross-coherence cells verified, attack-type 7-type RPS, R30-boss-clear sanity at 51.7s — have just survived a 4-round single-doc cross-coherence audit at zero cascade-violations. Re-scoping any of these would require reopening upstream locks and re-running per-civ / per-tower / per-God arcs. That is an enormous cost against a swarm finding (the supersession) that explicitly *did not* attack the unit/structure/tier substrate; it attacked seven-mode-breadth at solo polish bar, monetisation architecture, and product-shape narrative coherence.

The honest read: the supersession's load-bearing finding is that **breadth is unreachable**, not that **depth is wrong**. The unit/structure/tier substrate is the *depth* the swarm is implicitly preserving — Tower + PvP-Co-op feature-complete at 1.0 means the 3-civ × 6-tower × Fusion stack is exactly what ships, not what gets re-scoped. Re-scoping the substrate now would be the cascade-violation `CLAUDE.md` exists to prevent: a Phase-N decision (product-shape) silently editing Phase-N-1 constraints (unit / tower / civ specs already locked).

What the supersession *does* cleanly authorise: re-framing Promise 1 (north-star edit), reopening §4.8 item #9 (engine), filing the discipline-lock decisions, and dropping Mud-to-Myth from CONCEPT.md hub. Those are refinement work, not re-scope work. The re-scope framing applies to the published commitment, not to the design substrate.

## 3. The "what-we-do-better-vs-others-do-better" swarm proposal

If the PM authorises a targeted swarm on this question, the proposed shape:

**Question framing.** "Better" needs decomposition before agents touch it. Four candidate axes: (1) *polish* — execution quality at first contact (Stormgate's failure mode); (2) *mechanic* — load-bearing system design that is genuinely novel or non-trivially recombined (Fusion, attack-type RPS, per-civ identity-hook); (3) *scope-fit* — depth-per-dollar vs comparator at the post-cut Tower + PvP-Co-op shape; (4) *integration* — how the locked surfaces compose with each other (the cross-coherence audit's 36/36 finding is a real edge). The framing should commit to (2)-(3) as primary and (1)-(4) as secondary; (1) is unmeasurable pre-engine and (4) is internal-discipline-flavoured rather than player-felt.

**Comparator set.** Legion TD 2 (the Player-lens reference-class anchor; 87% positive across 6,350+ reviews); Bloons TD 6 (the polish-and-content ceiling; ongoing live-service); Element TD 2 (the WC3-lineage spiritual-successor case); SC2 Squadron TD (Map-Maker lineage at low-modern polish); Tower Defense Simulator (Roblox; the F2P lobby-discoverability comp); Mindustry (the substrate-and-modding case); Path of Achra (solo-dev depth-over-breadth shipped success); They Are Billions (RTS-TD hybrid with the canonical "add one mode-type post-EA" cost data point already in evidence base). Eight comparators; tractable for one round.

**Axes of comparison.** Units, structures, tiers, progression (commander leveling + Fusion + Tribute), lobby/multiplayer, monetisation, replay/observer, modding/custom-game surface, mode-roster breadth-vs-depth ratio. Nine axes × eight comparators is a 72-cell matrix — exactly the grain-size the recent per-tower / per-God audits handled cleanly.

**Swarm shape.** Smaller than the product-shape swarm. Three rounds, ~6 agents total: R1 = 3 comparator-audit agents (Legion+BTD+ETD2 / SC2+TDS / Mindustry+PoA+TaB); R2 = differentiation-thesis dialectician + counter-thesis dialectician (where AtoA is *actually* worse than the comparator); R3 = synthesiser closing thesis + counter-thesis into a load-bearing differentiation paragraph. One alignment-check between R1 and R2. Total cost: comparable to one R-round of the product-shape swarm.

**Output format.** Two artifacts. (i) `research/<date>-tw-comparator-matrix.md` — the 72-cell matrix as evidence substrate, kept as research not decision. (ii) `decisions/<date>-atoa-differentiation-thesis.md` — the load-bearing thesis paragraph plus 3-5 specific axes where AtoA does better and 2-3 where comparators do better, with explicit "we are not trying to win on these" framing. The latter feeds directly into the Steam-page draft chartered as follow-up #12 in SYNTHESIS.

## 4. Honest contrarian read

Running another swarm right now is plausibly *not* the right move. The strongest case for ratify-first-then-refine: the supersession decision sits at Status: Proposed; ratifying it is a single-step act that unblocks twelve downstream cascade items, including the scope-cut decision file (which is the natural carrier for the (A) / (C) work above) and the engine-lock arc (which is (B)). Running a differentiation swarm before ratification risks producing a thesis artifact that then has to be re-cut against whichever scope-pair the PM chooses, doubling the work. The supersession also hasn't been read by the PM yet at the moment this brief is being written; the swarm's converged recommendation is sitting in `SYNTHESIS.md` pending engagement.

The strongest case for swarm-now: the differentiation question is genuinely orthogonal to the scope-pair choice (the matrix would compare AtoA's substrate against comparators regardless of whether Tower+PvP-Co-op or Tower+Custom is the eventual pair), and Steam-page draft work needs the differentiation thesis as input. If the PM is mentally already past the supersession and looking for the next concrete arc, the differentiation swarm is the cheapest one to start.

Honest synthesis: ratification of the supersession should come first as a single-turn act. Once ratified, the (A) scope-pair decision is the cleanest next arc because it unblocks every other cascade item. The differentiation swarm (E) is the right *third* move, after (A) but parallel-safe with (B) engine-lock work. Running it second-or-earlier is plausible but trades cascade-discipline cleanliness for parallel velocity.
