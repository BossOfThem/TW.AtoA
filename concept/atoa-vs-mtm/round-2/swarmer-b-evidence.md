# Swarmer B Evidence — Paid Early Access (single-product) for solo-dev TW-genre

Round-2 evidence package feeding Arguer B. Brief: defend a single paid Early Access (~$10–20) Steam release as the strongest shape for AtoA, vs. a free HTML mirror + paid Steam two-product split.

Promises (for relevance scoring):
1. Solo-shippable + feature-complete TW-genre mode-type coverage at launch.
2. SC1/WC3-style custom-lobby customizability and recognizability.
3. Optional Battle Pass + Ranked Progression that doesn't compromise regular play.

---

  SOURCE: https://screenrant.com/stardew-valley-sales-data/
  STRENGTH: high
  CONFIDENCE: high
  COUNTER-EVIDENCE: Stardew was an outlier farming-sim, not a TW-genre/MP title; success rate is not generalizable.
  PROMISE-1 RELEVANCE (0-5): 4
  PROMISE-2 RELEVANCE (0-5): 1
  PROMISE-3 RELEVANCE (0-5): 1
  RELEVANCE NOTE: Strongest single-paid solo-dev existence proof; relevant primarily to "ship one product" feasibility (P1), not custom-lobby or BP shape.

This is the genre-adjacent precedent for the paid-EA single-product approach.

  CONFIDENCE: high

The paid Steam release of a previously-free product is a positive case, but key caveat: they kept the free legacy version separate, and the paid version had distinct value (graphics + music + Steam features).

## Honesty notes

- **Pico Park / Steam policy direction:** the "free → paid is impossible" lock is real on Steam, but AtoA's hypothetical free side is HTML-hosted, not Steam-hosted, so the lock-in argument is weaker than it first looks for the actual proposed split. Flagged in counter-evidence.
- **Dwarf Fortress is a partial double-edged sword:** it actually proves a free-product + paid-Steam-product split CAN work spectacularly. The honest read is that DF had 15 years of free-version brand equity first; a new solo dev cannot manufacture that runway, so the analogy doesn't transfer directly to AtoA's day-zero choice.
- **Stardew is the genre-mismatch caveat:** different genre, different audience expectations. Cited for shape-of-product proof, not for genre comp.
- **Tangy TD / Core Defense dollar figures:** quoted via roundup; would be worth a direct dev post-mortem cross-check before relying on them in the final argument.
- **Hypothesis that didn't pan out:** I went looking for a study explicitly comparing Steam algorithmic visibility for free vs paid products and could not find one. The "concentrate wishlists/CTR on one SKU" argument is inferred from general discoverability research, not measured directly. Flagged in 2WB-7.
